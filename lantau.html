Lantau Island

Hong Kong's largest island — the Big Buddha, monasteries, beaches, and the airport.

Home / Hong Kong / Lantau Island

City Overview

Lantau Island is twice the size of Hong Kong Island but much less developed. It's home to the iconic Tian Tan Buddha (Big Buddha), the Po Lin Monastery, the picturesque fishing village of Tai O, and Hong Kong's international airport. The island offers a perfect mix of spirituality, nature, and coastal charm.

📍 Location

West of Hong Kong Island. Connected by bridges, tunnels, and ferries. Airport is on Lantau.

🙏 Big Buddha

The 34m-tall Tian Tan Buddha — one of the world's largest seated bronze Buddha statues.

🏘️ Tai O Fishing Village

A charming stilt-house village on the west coast — famous for seafood and sunset views.

🎢 Disneyland

Hong Kong's theme park — compact but fun, with unique attractions not found elsewhere.

Top Attractions

🙏 Tian Tan Buddha

Climb the 268 steps to the Big Buddha — the views and the atmosphere are worth every step.

🏘️ Tai O Village

Walk through the stilt houses, try shrimp paste, and watch the sunset over the South China Sea.

🎢 Disneyland

Hong Kong's compact Disneyland — unique attractions like Mystic Manor and the painted avenue of stars.

🏖️ Cheung Sha Beach

Hong Kong's longest beach — a peaceful escape from the urban environment.

Best Time to Visit Lantau Island

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm and pleasant. Wildflowers on hiking trails. Buddha's birthday celebrations.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot. Beach season. Best for Tai O seafood and coastal activities.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable. Perfect for the Big Buddha hike and Tai O.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool. Quiet and peaceful. Few tourists on the island. Great for contemplative visits.

Lantau Island Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Ngong Ping 360: Take the cable car to the Big Buddha — the 25-minute ride offers stunning views. Book the crystal cabin for the best experience.
  • Tai O: Visit in the late afternoon — the stilt houses are beautiful, and the sunset over the water is spectacular.
  • Big Buddha: Climb the 268 steps. At the top, the view of Lantau's mountains and sea is breathtaking.
  • Combine: Do Ngong Ping 360 + Big Buddha + Tai O in one day — they're all on the same bus route.
