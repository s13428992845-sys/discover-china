Tibet (Xizang)

China's highest and most remote autonomous region, known as the "Roof of the World". Home to Tibetan Buddhism, the majestic Potala Palace, Mount Everest, and some of the most breathtaking landscapes on Earth. A once-in-a-lifetime destination for adventurous travelers.

Home / Provinces / Tibet

Province Overview

Tibet (Xizang) is China's highest and most remote autonomous region, covering 1.2 million square kilometers. Located on the Tibetan Plateau, it borders Nepal, Bhutan, India, and Myanmar. The region features an average elevation of 4,500 meters, making it the highest region on Earth outside Antarctica.

📍 Location

Southwest China, on the Tibetan Plateau. Borders Nepal, Bhutan, India, Myanmar.

👥 Population

~3.6 million, predominantly Tibetan. Rich Buddhist culture and traditions.

🌡️ Climate

High-altitude, dry and cold. Best visited May-October. Winter is extremely cold at high altitude.

🏔️ Terrain

Average elevation 4,500m+. Himalayas, Mount Everest (8,848m), vast grasslands, sacred lakes.

🍜 Cuisine

Tibetan butter tea, tsampa (roasted barley flour), yak meat dishes, momos (dumplings).

✈️ Major Airport

Lhasa Gonggar Airport (LXA). Also Nyingchi (LZY) and Shigatse (RKZ).

Why Visit Tibet?

🏛️ Potala Palace

The iconic 13-story palace of the Dalai Lamas. 1,000+ rooms, sacred Buddhist relics, and panoramic Lhasa views.

🏔️ Mount Everest

World's highest peak! Visit Base Camp at 5,200m for the closest view without climbing.

🙏 Tibetan Buddhism

Monasteries, prayer flags, pilgrims prostrating. Sera, Drepung, Tashilhunpo monasteries.

💎 Sacred Lakes

Namtso (highest salt lake at 4,718m), Yamdrok (turquoise jewel), and Manasarovar.

🏔️ Himalayan Landscapes

Unmatched mountain scenery. Everest, Shishapangma, and endless snow-capped peaks.

🎪 Unique Culture

Sky burial ceremonies, Thangka painting, Tibetan opera, and colorful festival celebrations.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming up, clear skies, fewer tourists. Some festivals.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warmest season, lush grasslands, but also rainy. Peak tourist season.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Oct)

BEST time. Clear skies, mild temperatures, stunning visibility of peaks.

❄️ Winter (Nov-Feb)

Very cold, some areas inaccessible. But dramatic landscapes and almost no tourists.

Getting Around Tibet

✈️ By Air

  • Major Airport: Lhasa Gonggar (LXA) from Chengdu, Beijing, etc.
  • Regional Airports: Nyingchi Airport for eastern Tibet
  • Domestic Flights: Essential for quick access to remote regions

🚄 By Train

  • Qinghai-Tibet Railway: From Xining (22hrs) — world's highest railway
  • Scenic Experience: Incredible scenery across the plateau
  • Comfort: Train helps with altitude acclimatization

🚗 By Car / 4WD

  • Hiring 4WD: Essential for most travel. Driver included
  • Road Conditions: Roads can be rough and high altitude
  • Flexibility: Best for exploring remote areas and scenic routes

📋 Organized Tours

  • Tibet Travel Permit: Required for all foreigners — arrange through travel agency
  • Most Visitors: Join organized tours for permit handling and logistics
  • Benefits: Local guides, accommodation, and transportation included

Tibet Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Tibet Travel Permit (TTP): Required for all foreigners — arrange through travel agency in advance.
  • Altitude Sickness: SERIOUS — spend 2-3 days acclimatizing in Lhasa (3,650m) before going higher.
  • Bring Medication: Diamox for altitude, sunscreen (UV is extreme at high altitude), lip balm.
  • Respect Religious Customs: Walk clockwise around temples, don't touch monks' heads, remove hats.
  • Photography Restrictions: At some monasteries — always ask permission.
  • Bring Cash: ATMs are scarce outside Lhasa, credit cards rarely accepted.
  • Pack Layers: Temperature can swing 20°C in a single day.
  • Oxygen Bags: Available in Lhasa pharmacies — helpful for altitude adjustment.
