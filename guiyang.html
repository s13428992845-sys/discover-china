Guiyang

The cool summer capital of Guizhou — a city surrounded by emerald karst mountains, rich Miao minority culture, and some of China's most underrated cuisine.

Home / Guizhou / Guiyang

📍 Top Attractions

Qianling Park (黔灵山公园)

A massive urban park with wild macaque monkeys, ancient temples, and a lake. The Hongfu Temple at the summit offers panoramic city views.

Qingyan Ancient Town (青岩古镇)

A 600-year-old Ming Dynasty military town with stone-paved streets, city walls, and traditional local snacks.

Jiaxiu Pavilion (甲秀楼)

Guiyang's iconic landmark — a three-story Ming Dynasty pavilion perched on a giant rock in the Nanming River.

🍜 Food & Cuisine

Guizhou cuisine is known for its sour and spicy flavors. Must-try dishes include Sour Soup Fish (酸汤鱼), Huaxi Beef Noodles (花溪牛肉粉), and Siwawa (丝娃娃) — fresh vegetable wraps served with dipping sauce.

💡 Travel Tips

  • Guiyang is known as the "Summer Resort Capital" — temperatures rarely exceed 28°C in summer
  • The city sits at 1,100m elevation — generally comfortable year-round
  • Guiyang is a gateway to Huangguoshu Waterfall and Miao villages
  • High-speed rail connects Guiyang to Guangzhou (~4h) and Kunming (~2h)
