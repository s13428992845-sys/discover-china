🏨 Budget Accommodation

China offers excellent budget accommodation. International chains like hostelels and guesthouses provide great value for travelers.

Hostels ($10-30/night)

🏠 Hostels (青年旅舍)

Dorm: $8-15/night

Private room: $20-35/night

Dorms of 4-8 beds, common areas, sometimes kitchens. Great for meeting other travelers.

🏨 Budget Hotels (经济型酒店)

Single: $20-40/night

Double: $25-50/night

Clean, basic rooms. Chains like Motel 168, 7 Days Inn. No-frills but reliable.

🏡 Guesthouses (民宿)

Range: $25-80/night

Locations: Old towns, rural areas, scenic regions

Local family-run, more authentic experience. Book via Tujia or Airbnb.

💡 Booking Tips

  • Agoda: Often cheapest for China
  • 携程 (Trip.com): Best local selection
  • Booking.com: Good for English interface
  • Walk-in: Sometimes cheaper for budget hotels
  • Location: Stay near metro for convenience

🍜 Food & Dining

Chinese food is incredibly cheap outside tourist areas. Eat like a local and save big.

Where to Eat

🍜 Local Restaurants

Budget: ¥15-40/person ($2-6)

Find crowded local spots (good sign). Point at what others are eating. Learn "Zhege" (this) and "Duo shao qian" (how much).

🥡 Fast Food / Noodle Shops

Budget: ¥10-25/person ($1.50-4)

Lamian (hand-pulled noodles), dumplings, congee. Point-and-order menus or picture menus.

🛒 Markets & Street Food

Budget: ¥5-30 per meal ($0.75-4)

Night markets offer variety and cheap eats. Try everything! Start with ¥10-20 experiments.

🏪 Convenience Stores

Budget: ¥10-30 ($1.50-4)

7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson. Hot food, ready meals, sandwiches. Open 24/7.

Budget Meals by City

City Cheap Eats Where to Find
Beijing ¥15-30 Wangfujing night market, hole-in-the-wall spots
Shanghai ¥20-40 Nanjing Road, Yu Garden area
Xi'an ¥10-25 Muslim Quarter, local restaurants
Chengdu ¥15-30 Jinli Street, local hot pot spots
Guilin ¥10-25 East-West Street, local noodle shops

🍽️ Eating Tips

  • Avoid tourist areas: Prices triple within 1 block of attractions
  • Look for Chinese characters: "小吃" (snacks), "面馆" (noodle shop), "饭馆" (restaurant)
  • Water: Bring reusable bottle, fill at hotel, or buy large bottles at convenience stores
  • Meals: Breakfast ¥5-15, Lunch ¥15-30, Dinner ¥20-50 for budget travelers

🚄 Transportation

China's transportation is efficient and affordable. Mastering public transit will save you money.

Getting Around Cities

🚇 Metro

Cost: ¥2-8 per ride

Excellent in major cities. English signs in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou. Buy single-ride tickets or reloadable cards.

🚌 Bus

Cost: ¥1-5 per ride

Cheapest option but confusing. Use Google Maps or Baidu Maps for routes. Prepare small bills.

🚗 Didi (滴滴)

Cost: ¥8-50 for short trips

Like Uber. Use for late nights or when metro closes. Cash or WeChat/Alipay payment.

Between Cities

🚄 High-Speed Rail

Second Class: ¥0.12-0.15/km

Best value for speed. Book 30 days ahead on Trip.com. Beijing-Shanghai: ¥553 (~$75) for 4.5 hours.

✈️ Flights

When to book: 2-3 months ahead

Compare with rail. Sometimes cheaper than expected, sometimes more. Check Ctrip, Trip.com.

🚌 Long-Distance Bus

Cost: ¥0.10-0.20/km

Cheapest option for long distances. Slow but comfortable. Overnight buses save accommodation.

🚂 Slow Trains

Hard seat/sleeper: ¥0.06-0.10/km

Z, T, K trains. Much cheaper than high-speed. Overnight options save time and money.

🎫 Attractions & Activities

Many Chinese attractions are overpriced. Here is how to maximize experiences while minimizing costs.

Free or Cheap Attractions

🏯 Free Attractions

• Tiananmen Square (Beijing)

• Former French Concession (Shanghai)

• Local parks and temples

• City walking tours

• Street markets

🎟️ Discounted Tickets

• Book online (often 5-10% off)

• Student/youth cards (if applicable)

• Free entry days (varies by city)

• Combo tickets for multiple sites

Major Attraction Costs

Panda Base (Chengdu) ¥58 Go early morning for active pandas

💳 Money & Payments

Managing money in China is different from other countries. Cash and mobile payments dominate.

💰 Payment Methods

  • WeChat Pay / Alipay: Essential for everything. Link international card or open Chinese account
  • Cash: Bring USD/EUR, exchange at banks or use ATMs
  • ATMs: Available everywhere, ¥10-20 fee per withdrawal
  • Credit Cards: Accepted at hotels and upscale restaurants only
  • Wise / Revolut: Good exchange rates for card spending

💱 Money-Saving Tips

  • Exchange cash: Better rates at Bank of China than airports
  • ATMs: Use CITIC, ICBC, or ABC (lower fees)
  • Small towns: Carry more cash, fewer card options
  • Negotiate: Expected at markets, start at 40-50% of asking price

📊 Sample Budget Itineraries

🥉 Budget Backpacker ($50/day)

  • Accommodation: ¥80-150 dorm or cheap hotel
  • Food: ¥40-60 local meals, markets, convenience stores
  • Transport: Metro, buses, slow trains, overnight journeys
  • Attractions: 1 major + free sights per day
  • Total: ¥350-400/day (~$50)

🥈 Comfortable Traveler ($100/day)

  • Accommodation: ¥200-350 budget hotel or hostel private
  • Food: ¥80-150 mix of local and nicer restaurants
  • Transport: Metro, occasional Didi, high-speed rail
  • Attractions: 2 major sites per day
  • Total: ¥700-900/day (~$100)

🥇 Full Comfort ($200+/day)

  • Accommodation: ¥500-1000+ mid-range hotel
  • Food: ¥200-400 fine dining and experiences
  • Transport: Didi everywhere, flights between cities
  • Attractions: All major sites, private guides
  • Total: ¥1500-2500+/day (~$200+)

💡 Ultimate Money-Saving Tips

  • Travel shoulder season: November-March (not CNY) offers 30-50% savings
  • Book ahead: Attractions and trains are cheaper when booked early
  • Visit Yunnan: Cheaper than Beijing/Shanghai, beautiful year-round
  • Free walking tours: Available in most major cities
  • Tea houses: ¥10-30 for hours of relaxation (cheaper than bars)
  • SIM cards: Buy at airport or convenience store (¥30-100 for data)
  • Water: Buy large bottles at convenience stores, not tourist shops
  • Shopping: Head to local markets, not tourist markets

⚠️ What NOT to Skimp On

  • Train tickets: Buy second class, not standing room
  • Train station proximity: Save time, stay near transport hubs
  • Panda Base: Go early, this is worth the full price
  • Great Wall: Mutianyu or Simatai, not Badaling
  • One nice meal: Splurge on authentic Peking Duck or hot pot once

China is one of the best value destinations for travelers. With some planning and local know-how, you can experience world-class attractions, incredible food, and unique culture without breaking the bank.
