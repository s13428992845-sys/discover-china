Guangzhou

The ancient trading port and dim sum capital — 2,200 years of commerce, cuisine, and culture.

Home / Guangdong Province / Guangzhou

City Overview

Guangzhou (Canton) is one of China's oldest and most important cities. As the starting point of the Maritime Silk Road and the host of the Canton Fair, it has been a center of international trade for over 2,000 years. Today it's famous worldwide for Cantonese cuisine, especially dim sum morning tea.

📍 Location

Southern China, Pearl River Delta. 30 min by HSR from Hong Kong.

🏙️ Canton Tower

Guangzhou's 600m landmark — one of the tallest towers in the world with stunning observation decks.

🥟 Dim Sum Capital

The birthplace of Cantonese dim sum — morning tea (早茶) is a cherished daily ritual.

🏛️ Chen Clan Academy

The finest example of Lingnan architecture — exquisite wood, stone, and brick carvings from 1894.

Top Attractions

🏙️ Canton Tower

Ascend to the 450m observation deck — or ride the Ferris wheel on top for a unique experience.

🏮 Shamian Island

A sandbank island of colonial European architecture — a peaceful escape from the busy city.

🏛️ Chen Clan Academy

Now the Guangdong Folk Art Museum — the most ornate traditional building in southern China.

🌺 Yuexiu Park

Guangzhou's largest park — home to the Five Rams Statue, the city's symbol.

Best Time to Visit Guangzhou

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm and humid. Flowers in full bloom. Best for parks and outdoor dining.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Very hot and humid. Typhoon season. Indoor attractions and air-conditioned restaurants are best.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Cool, dry, comfortable. Perfect for exploring the city on foot.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Mild (10-20°C). Comfortable for sightseeing. Occasional cold snaps.

Guangzhou Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Morning Tea: Visit a traditional tea house like Guangzhou Restaurant (广州酒家) before 10am for the freshest dim sum.
  • Canton Fair: If visiting during April or October, expect huge crowds and high hotel prices — but the energy is incredible.
  • Shamian Island: A 2-hour walking tour of this former concession area reveals beautiful colonial architecture.
  • Night Market: Beijing Road pedestrian street comes alive at night — shopping, street food, and entertainment.
