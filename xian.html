Xi'an

Ancient capital of 13 dynasties — home of the world-famous Terracotta Warriors, a complete Ming Dynasty city wall, and the eastern terminus of the Silk Road.

Home / Shaanxi / Xi'an

📍 Top Attractions

Terracotta Warriors (兵马俑)

One of the world's greatest archaeological discoveries — over 8,000 life-sized warrior statues guarding Emperor Qin Shi Huang's tomb for 2,200 years.

Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)

The most complete ancient city wall in China — cycle the entire 13.7km atop this magnificent Ming Dynasty fortification.

Muslim Quarter (回民街)

A labyrinth of food stalls and shops in the historic Hui Muslim district — Xi'an's best street food destination.

🍜 Must-Try Food

  • Roujiamo (肉夹馍) — China's original "burger": shredded meat in crispy flatbread
  • Biangbiang Noodles (biangbiang面) — Thick, belt-like noodles with chili oil
  • Yangrou Paomo (羊肉泡馍) — Crumbled flatbread in lamb soup
  • Liangpi (凉皮) — Cold skin noodles, perfect for summer

🚄 Getting There

✈️ By Air

  • Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY)
  • Direct flights from major Asian and European cities

🚄 By High-Speed Rail

  • Beijing to Xi'an: ~4.5 hours
  • Chengdu to Xi'an: ~3.5 hours
  • Shanghai to Xi'an: ~6 hours

🚇 Local Transport

  • 9 metro lines covering the city
  • Bus #306 to Terracotta Warriors from Railway Station

💡 Travel Tips

  • Best time: March–May and September–November
  • Buy Terracotta Warriors tickets online in advance — it gets very crowded
  • Rent a bike to ride atop the City Wall at sunset
  • Be adventurous with street food in the Muslim Quarter
