Inner Mongolia Grasslands

Endless rolling green plains beneath impossibly blue skies — sleep in a traditional yurt, gallop across the steppe on horseback, and experience true nomadic culture.

Home / Inner Mongolia / Grasslands

📍 Top Grasslands

Xilamuren Grassland (希拉穆仁草原)

The most accessible grassland from Hohhot (~90 min). Traditional Mongolian welcome ceremony, yurt camps, and horse riding across rolling hills.

Huitengxile Grassland (辉腾锡勒草原)

Higher elevation grassland (~2,000m) with cooler temperatures and wind turbines dotting the landscape. Beautiful alpine meadows and wildflowers in summer.

Gegen Tala Grassland (格根塔拉草原)

One of Inner Mongolia's most famous grasslands — the main venue for the annual Naadam Festival featuring wrestling, archery, and horse racing.

🐎 Activities

  • Horseback Riding: ¥150-300 per hour — ride across the grasslands with Mongolian guides
  • Yurt (Ger) Stay: ¥200-500/night — sleep in a traditional felt yurt, wake up to endless skies
  • Camel Riding: Available at some grassland resorts
  • Bonfire Party: Most camps organize evening bonfires with Mongolian song and dance
  • Naadam Festival: Mid-July — Mongolia's Olympics with wrestling, archery, and horse racing

🍜 Grassland Cuisine

  • Roast Whole Lamb (烤全羊) — The ultimate Mongolian feast
  • Hand-Grasped Mutton (手把肉) — Boiled lamb eaten with hands
  • Mongolian Milk Tea (奶茶) — Salty, creamy, and warming
  • Kumis (马奶酒) — Fermented mare's milk

💡 Travel Tips

  • Best season: Mid-June to late August — grass is greenest, weather is warm
  • Book during Naadam Festival (July 11-13) for the full cultural experience
  • Bring warm clothing even in summer — nights on the steppe can be cold
  • Expect basic facilities at yurt camps — this is a rustic, authentic experience
  • Most grassland areas are 1.5-3 hours from Hohhot by car
