Kunming

The "Spring City" — year-round mild climate, blooming flowers, and the gateway to Yunnan's diverse attractions. China's most livable city awaits you.

Home / Yunnan / Kunming

City Overview

📍 Location

Capital of Yunnan Province, located in central Yunnan at 1,890 meters elevation. Hub for flights and trains to all destinations.

👥 Population

Approximately 8.5 million in the metropolitan area. Cosmopolitan and modern city with friendly locals.

🌡️ Climate

Mild year-round (10-25°C average). Called "Spring City" — no extreme summers or winters. Best: Mar-May, Sep-Nov.

🏛️ Character

Modern city with Chinese traits, beautiful parks, flower markets, and excellent food. Clean streets and good infrastructure.

💡 Why Visit Kunming?

  • Gateway: Most flights to Yunnan land here. Perfect first or last stop.
  • Flowers: Dajing Pavilion flower market, Yunnan's "Flower Capital."
  • Gateway to Stone Forest: UNESCO site just 90km away — easy day trip.
  • Affordable: One of China's most affordable provincial capitals for accommodation and food.
  • Day Trips: Jiuzhaigou, Xishan, Dianchi Lake, and ethnic villages nearby.

Top Attractions

Stone Forest (Shilin)

One of China's most famous natural wonders — towering limestone pillars that look like petrified trees. Legend says this is where the beauty of a local girl turned to stone to escape an evil emperor. Covering 400+ sq km with countless formations, allow a full day to explore.

💰 Ticket: ¥130 (Peak) / ¥65 (Off-peak)
🕐 Open: 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Distance: ~90 km from city center
UNESCO Day Trip Nature Photography
Cuihu Park

Green Lake Park (Cuihu Gongyuan)

Urban oasis in the city center. Locals practice tai chi, dance, and play instruments around the lake. Beautiful in all seasons, especially when lotus flowers bloom in summer. Enjoy traditional tea houses and street food vendors around the perimeter.

💰 Ticket: Free
🕐 Open: Open 24 hours
📍 Location: City Center
Free Local Life Relaxing
Yuantong Temple

Yuantong Temple (Yuantong Si)

Yunnan's largest Buddhist temple, dating back 1,200+ years. Features a unique water-flowing design where the main hall sits at the center of a pond connected by a bridge. A peaceful retreat for meditation and appreciating traditional architecture.

💰 Ticket: ¥6
🕐 Open: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Location: North of center
Buddhism History Culture
Dounan Flower Market

Dounan Flower Market

Asia's largest flower market — over 80% of China's fresh cut flowers pass through here. Wholesale prices available to the public. Best time to visit is late night to early morning when flowers arrive. A visual feast of colors and fragrant scents.

💰 Ticket: Free
🕐 Best Time: Midnight - 6 AM
📍 Location: Chengdu Road
Flowers Wholesale Photo Spot
Dianchi Lake

Dianchi Lake (Dian Chi)

Large lake south of the city, known as the "Pearl of the Plateau." Haigeng Park offers lakeside walks and paddle boats. The boardwalk is popular at sunset. Combined with a visit to Xishan (Western Hills) for panoramic views.

💰 Ticket: Free (park); Cable car ¥70
🕐 Open: Park open 24 hours
📍 Location: South of city
Lake Sunset Boating

Local Food & Restaurants

Kunming's cuisine reflects Yunnan's ethnic diversity with mild flavors and fresh ingredients

🍜

Crossing-Bridge Noodles (Guoqiao Mixian)

The iconic Yunnan dish — steaming broth, raw ingredients you cook yourself at the table. Served with rice noodles, thin meat slices, vegetables, and chili oil. Best at old establishments like "Maojia."

🍄

Wild Mushrooms (Ye Sheng Zhu)

Yunnan is China's mushroom capital. Try morels, chanterelles, and the prized matsutake. June-October is mushroom season. Head to "Mushroom King" or night markets for fresh selections.

🥟

Flowering Silkworm (Zhenghua Cun)

Botanic vegetables unique to Yunnan including steam-fried flowers, ferns, and edible weeds. Yunnan has over 600 edible plant species — a vegetarian paradise.

🥘

Yunnan Ham (Huaxi Feu)

Cured ham from eastern Yunnan, similar to Spanish serrano. Thinly sliced and eaten cold, or cooked with vegetables. Paired with fresh pears for a local appetizer.

🍵

Pu'er Tea

Yunnan's famous fermented tea — visit tea houses for tasting sessions. The "Pu'er King" and "Wangchao Tea City" offer everything from ¥10 samples to aged varieties worth thousands.

🍽️ Where to Eat

  • Nanping Street: Popular pedestrian food street with local snacks
  • Wenhua Lane: Trendy cafes and modern Yunnan cuisine
  • Decai Food Street: Night market with variety of options
  • Dounan Flower Market area: Late-night snacks and local favorites

Transportation Guide

✈️ Getting to Kunming

  • Kunming Changshui Airport (KMG): Major international hub. Metro Line 6 connects to center (¥5, 45 min).
  • From Beijing/Shanghai: ~3 hours by air. Daily trains available (30+ hours).
  • High-speed trains: To Dali (2 hrs), Lijiang (3 hrs), Guangzhou (6 hrs).

🚇 Getting Around

  • Metro: 6 lines covering major attractions. ¥2-8 per ride. Clean and efficient.
  • Didi (滴滴): Use WeChat or Alipay. Most reliable taxi option. ¥10-40 within city.
  • Bus: Extensive network, ¥2 for most rides. Good for experiencing local life.
  • Bicycle: Hello Bike and Meituan bikes throughout city. ¥1-2 per ride.

🚌 Day Trips

  • Stone Forest: Bus from East Bus Station (¥30, 1.5 hrs) or organized tour (¥200-400).
  • Jiuzhaigou: Scenic village, 1.5 hrs by bus from South Bus Station.
  • Xishan (Western Hills): Bus #94 or cable car from Dian Lake (¥70).

💡 Tips

  • Download "Didi" app before arriving. Register with phone number.
  • Metro is fastest during rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM).
  • Airport has English signs. Metro has English announcements.
  • Taxi drivers often don't speak English — show destination in Chinese.
