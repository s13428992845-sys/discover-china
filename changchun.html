Changchun

China's 'Film City' and automobile capital — imperial history meets modern innovation.

Home / Jilin Province / Changchun

City Overview

Changchun, the capital of Jilin, has a unique identity as China's film and automotive industries hub. It was also the capital of the Japanese puppet state of Manchukuo (1932-1945), and the Puppet Manchukuo Palace remains as a sobering museum. Today it's a green, spacious city with wide boulevards and a thriving cultural scene.

📍 Location

Central Jilin Province. HSR hub connecting Harbin (1h), Shenyang (1.5h), Beijing (4h).

🎬 Film City

The 'Hollywood of the East' — home to Changchun Film Studio, China's oldest film studio.

🚗 Auto Capital

China's first automobile factory (FAW) is here — the birthplace of China's car industry.

🏛️ Puppet Palace

The former palace of Puyi, China's last emperor — a deeply moving museum of Japanese occupation.

Top Attractions

🏛️ Puppet Manchukuo Palace

A sobering museum documenting Puyi's life as a puppet emperor under Japanese control.

🎬 Changchun Film Studio

Visit the 'Hollywood of the East' — see how Chinese movies are made and explore film history.

🌿 Jingyuetan

A massive forest park on the city's edge — 'the lung of Changchun.' Beautiful in all seasons.

🏛️ Museum of the War of Resistence

A comprehensive museum covering China's struggle against Japanese invasion.

Best Time to Visit Changchun

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Rapid warming. May is pleasant. Trees leaf out quickly. Good for park visits.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warm and pleasant (22-28°C). The best time for outdoor activities and park visits.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Oct)

Beautiful fall colors. Jingyuetan forest is spectacular. Comfortable temperatures.

❄️ Winter (Nov-Mar)

Very cold (-20 to -10°C). Ice sculptures. Indoor museums and hot food are the focus.

Changchun Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Puppet Palace: Allow 2-3 hours — the exhibitions are extensive and deeply moving. An essential part of understanding 20th-century China.
  • Jingyuetan: Rent a bike to explore this massive forest park — it's 90 square km, larger than many cities.
  • Food: Changchun is famous for 朝鲜族 (Korean ethnic) cuisine — try cold noodles, kimchi, and rice cake soup.
  • Skiing: Changchun's nearby ski resorts (Lianhuashan, Miaoxiang Mountain) are good for beginners and intermediates.
