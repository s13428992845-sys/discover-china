Jingdezhen

The Porcelain Capital — 1,700 years of ceramic art, ancient kilns, and living craft traditions.

Home / Jiangxi Province / Jingdezhen

City Overview

Jingdezhen has been China's porcelain capital for over 1,700 years. The city's imperial kilns produced the finest ceramics for Chinese emperors, and today the tradition continues with hundreds of workshops and studios creating exquisite pottery.

📍 Location

Northeastern Jiangxi, surrounded by hills rich in porcelain clay and kaolin.

🏺 Porcelain Capital

The world's ceramic capital for 1,700 years — imperial kilns supplied the Chinese court.

🎨 Living Tradition

Hundreds of workshops and studios — watch artisans create porcelain using ancient techniques.

🏛️ Imperial Kiln Museum

A stunning modern museum designed by architect Zhu Pei — blends ancient and contemporary.

Top Attractions

🏺 Ancient Kiln Site

The imperial kiln ruins — where emperors' porcelain was made for 500+ years. Now a museum.

🎨 Workshop Visit

Visit a working porcelain workshop — watch the entire process from clay to finished piece.

🏛️ Imperial Kiln Museum

Award-winning architecture housing priceless porcelain. A must-visit for art lovers.

🛍️ Pottery Market

The weekend pottery market — browse thousands of pieces from traditional to avant-garde.

Best Time to Visit Jingdezhen

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Pleasant weather. Good for workshop visits and outdoor kiln sites.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot. Best for indoor museum and workshop visits. Pottery markets continue.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Comfortable temperatures. Ideal for exploring workshops and markets.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold (2-8°C). Quiet season for workshops — perfect for intimate studio visits.

Jingdezhen Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Ancient Kiln Site: Start here to understand 1,700 years of porcelain history — the imperial kiln ruins are awe-inspiring.
  • Workshop Visit: Visit at least one working workshop — watching artisans shape porcelain is mesmerizing.
  • Pottery Market: The weekend pottery market (Saturday-Sunday) has thousands of pieces — from ¥10 to ¥10,000+.
  • Imperial Kiln Museum: Designed by Zhu Pei — the architecture alone is worth the visit. Go in the afternoon for best light.
