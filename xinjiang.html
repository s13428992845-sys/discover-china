Xinjiang

China's largest autonomous region, located in the northwest. A land of extraordinary contrasts — vast deserts, snow-capped mountains, ancient Silk Road cities, and vibrant Central Asian culture. Home to 47 ethnic groups including Uyghur, Kazakh, and Tajik peoples.

Home / Provinces / Xinjiang

Province Overview

Xinjiang is China's largest autonomous region, covering 1.66 million square kilometers — one-sixth of China's total land area. Located in the northwest, it borders 8 countries. The region features dramatic contrasts — from the scorching Taklamakan Desert to the snow-capped Tianshan Mountains and lush oases.

📍 Location

Northwest China, bordering 8 countries (Mongolia, Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Tajikistan, Afghanistan, Pakistan, India).

👥 Population

~26 million, with 47 ethnic groups. Uyghur, Kazakh, Han Chinese, and more.

🌡️ Climate

Extreme continental — scorching summers, freezing winters. Best visited May-October.

🏔️ Terrain

"Three mountains embrace two basins" — Altai, Tianshan, Kunlun mountains; Junggar and Tarim basins. Taklamakan Desert.

🍜 Cuisine

Uyghur cuisine — lamb kebabs, pilaf (polo), naan bread, laghman noodles, fresh fruits and nuts.

✈️ Major Airport

Urumqi Diwopu International Airport (URC). Also Kashgar (KHG), Turpan (TLQ).

Why Visit Xinjiang?

🏜️ Silk Road Heritage

2,000-year-old trade route cities. Kashgar Old City, Turpan ruins, ancient bazaars full of spices and crafts.

🏔️ Tianshan Mountains

UNESCO World Heritage. Heavenly Lake (Tianchi), alpine meadows, and pristine wilderness.

🐪 Taklamakan Desert

One of world's largest shifting-sand deserts. Camel rides, desert camping, and oases.

🍇 Turpan Oasis

Ancient Silk Road city. Grape valleys, Flaming Mountains, underground irrigation (karez), and ruins of Jiaohe.

🎭 Living Cultural Mosaic

Uyghur bazaars, Kazakh yurt camps, Tajik eagle dances. Central Asian culture within China.

🍈 Fruit Paradise

China's fruit basket. Hami melons, Turpan grapes, Kashgar pomegranates, and Ili lavender fields.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming up, wildflowers in Ili Valley. Some areas still cold.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot in deserts but perfect for northern routes. Fruit season! Lavender in June.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Oct)

BEST time. Comfortable temperatures, harvest season, golden landscapes.

❄️ Winter (Nov-Feb)

Extremely cold (-20°C in north). Ski season near Urumqi. Very few tourists.

Getting Around Xinjiang

✈️ By Air

  • Major Hub: Urumqi (URC) is major hub. Kashgar, Turpan, Ili also have airports
  • Domestic Flights: Essential for long-distance travel within the region
  • Time-Saving: Flights are recommended for connecting far destinations

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Urumqi connected to Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an
  • Regional Lines: Urumqi-Kashgar line connects southern Xinjiang
  • Scenic Routes: Experience diverse landscapes across the region

🚗 By Car / 4WD

  • Driver/4WD: Hiring recommended for long distances. Distances are vast
  • Flexibility: Best for exploring remote areas and scenic routes
  • Road Conditions: Major highways good, remote roads challenging

🚌 By Bus

  • Public Buses: Connect major towns
  • Sleeper Buses: Comfortable sleeper buses for long routes
  • Affordable: Budget-friendly option for independent travelers

Xinjiang Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Distances are HUGE: Xinjiang is 1/6 of China's land area. Plan travel days between cities.
  • Security Checks: Frequent — always carry passport and Chinese visa. Allow extra time.
  • Alcohol and Pork Sensitivity: Xinjiang is predominantly Muslim. Respect dietary customs.
  • Bargain at Bazaars: It's expected and part of the fun. Start at 50% of asking price.
  • Sun Protection: Essential — desert sun is intense, especially in Turpan.
  • Bring Layers: Temperature extremes between day and night, especially in desert areas.
  • Try the Naan Bread: Fresh from the oven — best food in all of China according to many travelers.
  • Learn Basic Uyghur Greetings: Locals appreciate the effort and it opens doors.
  • Cash is Important: In rural areas — mobile payment less common than in eastern China.
  • Travel with a Guide: Best for remote areas — language barrier and navigation can be challenging.
