Kowloon

Hong Kong's vibrant mainland side — street markets, neon lights, and the famous Avenue of Stars.

Home / Hong Kong / Kowloon

City Overview

Kowloon is Hong Kong's mainland counterpart — denser, grittier, and more authentically local than the island. Home to the famous Temple Street Night Market, the Avenue of Stars waterfront, and the colorful streets of Mong Kok, it offers the most intense urban experience in Hong Kong.

📍 Location

The southern peninsula of mainland Hong Kong. Connected to HK Island by MTR and Star Ferry.

🌃 Avenue of Stars

The iconic waterfront promenade — the best viewpoint for the Hong Kong skyline and Symphony of Lights.

🏮 Temple Street Night Market

Hong Kong's most famous night market — food stalls, fortune tellers, and bargaining.

🏙️ Mong Kok

The world's most densely populated district — a sensory overload of shops, food, and energy.

Top Attractions

🌃 Avenue of Stars

Watch the nightly Symphony of Lights show (8pm) — the world's largest permanent light and sound show.

🏮 Temple Street

Visit after 8pm — try street food, get your fortune told, and soak up the atmospheric neon glow.

🏙️ Mong Kok Markets

Explore the Ladies' Market, Goldfish Street, and Flower Market — each with unique character.

🚢 Star Ferry

The iconic cross-harbour ferry — one of the world's best value scenic rides ($0.50 HKD).

Best Time to Visit Kowloon

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm and humid. Pleasant for waterfront walks. Some fog possible.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid. Evening markets and waterfront come alive. Typhoon risk.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable. Perfect for the Symphony of Lights and outdoor markets.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool and dry. Great for walking tours and market exploration. Fewer tourists.

Kowloon Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Symphony of Lights: Watch from the Avenue of Stars at 8pm nightly — the Hong Kong skyline lights up in a spectacular show.
  • Star Ferry: Don't miss this 100-year-old institution — the $0.50 ride offers the best harbour views.
  • Street Food: Kowloon has the best street food in Hong Kong — try curry fish balls, egg waffles, and claypot rice.
  • Mong Kok: Visit the Ladies' Market (Tung Choi Street) for souvenirs and the Flower Market for a unique experience.
