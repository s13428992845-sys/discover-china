China High-Speed Rail Guide: How to Travel Like a Local

🚄 Master the world's largest high-speed rail network — 42,000km and counting

🚃 Train Types & Classes

China's rail system uses a letter-number system to identify train types. Understanding this helps you choose the right option.

Train Types

🚄 G-Trains (High-Speed)

Speed: 250-350 km/h

Best for: Long-distance premium travel

Price Range: ¥500-1,500+

Top-tier service, fastest option

🚄 C-Trains (Intercity)

Speed: 200-300 km/h

Best for: Short regional routes

Price Range: ¥100-400

Fast shuttles between cities

🚅 D-Trains (Express)

Speed: 200-250 km/h

Best for: Comfortable mid-range

Price Range: ¥300-800

Widely available, good value

🚃 Z/T/K Trains (Regular)

Speed: 80-160 km/h

Best for: Budget travelers

Price Range: ¥100-500

Slower but cheaper options

Seat Classes

💺 Second Class (二等座)

Standard seating, 5 seats across. Legroom is adequate. Best value for most travelers. Perfectly comfortable for 4-5 hour journeys.

💺 First Class (一等座)

4 seats across with more legroom. Wider seats, better cushions, power outlets at every seat. Worth the upgrade for longer journeys.

💺 Business Class (商务座)

3 seats across, lie-flat option on some trains. Premium service, meals included. Best for 8+ hour journeys or those who want maximum comfort.

🛏️ Sleeper Options

Available on overnight D and Z trains. Soft sleepers (4 berths), hard sleepers (6 berths), and reclining seats. Great for long distances.

🎫 Booking Tickets

Best Apps & Websites

Language: English

Payment: International cards accepted

Best for: Foreign visitors

Easy booking, small booking fee

📱 12306 (Official)

Language: Chinese only

Payment: Chinese bank card needed

Best for: Domestic travelers

No booking fee, can be tricky

📱 WeChat / Alipay

Language: Chinese only

Payment: WeChat Pay/Alipay

Best for: Residents

Integrated with transit cards

Booking Tips

  • Book Early: 30 days in advance for popular routes (Beijing-Shanghai, Beijing-Xi'an)
  • Golden Week: Book 2-3 months ahead for Chinese holidays
  • Morning Trains: Most popular, sell out first
  • ID Required: Have your passport ready for booking and boarding
  • Same-Day: Sometimes available at stations if online sold out

🏢 Station Navigation

Major Chinese train stations are massive and can be overwhelming. Here's how to navigate them:

Station Layout

🚪 Entrance/Exit

Usually separate. Look for "进站口" (Enter) and "出站口" (Exit). Ticket check at entrance.

🎫 Ticket Hall

Where you collect tickets if pre-booked, or buy same-day tickets. Has ticket machines and counters.

📋 Security Check

Baggage scanning similar to airports. Have ticket and ID ready. Liquids okay (unlike flights).

🪑 Waiting Hall

Large seating areas. Numbers on tickets correspond to waiting areas. Check departure boards.

🚉 Platform

Stairs/escalators down to platforms. Numbered platforms. Check your carriage number location.

🚶 Finding Your Carriage

Platform signs show where each carriage stops. Carriage 1 is at the front, 16 at the rear (varies by train length). First class is usually at the front, business class even further forward.

🚉 Boarding Process

Step 1: Arrive Early

Arrive 45-60 minutes before departure. Major stations are large and security takes time.

Step 2: Collect Tickets

Use "Ticket Collection" machines (刷身份证取票) with passport and booking number, or go to counter.

Step 3: Security Check

Proceed through security. Put bags through scanner, walk through metal detector. No restriction on liquids!

Step 4: Wait in Hall

Check departure board for platform (站台). Waiting halls have numbered areas matching your ticket.

Step 5: Boarding Call

Listen for boarding announcements (English on some trains). Board when platform is called for your train.

Step 6: Find Your Seat

Carriage and seat numbers clearly marked. Stow luggage above seats or at end of carriage.

🛋️ Onboard Amenities

🔌 Power Outlets

Every seat has USB or AC outlets. Bring a power bank as backup. Chinese plugs (Type A/I).

🚽 Restrooms

Clean toilets at carriage ends. Western-style in newer trains. Bring tissue (not always provided).

🍵 Dining Car

Food car serves instant noodles, rice dishes, snacks. Prices higher than outside. Bring your own snacks.

📶 WiFi

Free WiFi on most G-trains. Login with phone number. Can be slow during peak times.

🌡️ Climate Control

Central AC throughout. Can get cold — bring a layer even in summer.

🎥 Entertainment

Screens in newer first/business class. Local channels only for economy.

💡 Pro Tips

🎒 Luggage

  • Size Limit: 130cm combined dimensions (usually 28" suitcase)
  • Weight: 20kg for standard, 35kg for business class
  • Storage: Overhead bins or end-of-carriage racks
  • Valuables: Keep passport and electronics with you

🚄 For Families

  • Children: Under 6 free (no seat), 6-14 half price
  • Carriages: Family carriages available on some trains
  • Strollers: Can be stored at carriage ends
  • Feeding: Breastfeeding is accepted anywhere

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • No Smoking: Absolutely forbidden, including e-cigarettes
  • Keep Tickets: Needed for exit at destination
  • On Time: Trains leave exactly on time — be aboard early
  • Punctuality: China Railway is extremely reliable
