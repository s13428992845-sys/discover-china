Mohe

China's 'North Pole' — the northernmost city, extreme cold, and the chance to see Northern Lights.

Home / Heilongjiang Province / Mohe

City Overview

Mohe is the northernmost city in China — literally the 'North Pole' of the country. At 53°N latitude, it experiences extreme cold (down to -50°C), very long summer days (nearly 20 hours of daylight), and the remote possibility of seeing the Northern Lights. It's a unique frontier destination for the adventurous.

📍 Location

The northernmost tip of China, on the Amur River. Overnight train from Harbin (12h).

🧭 China's North Pole

The northernmost point of China — a unique geographic milestone with a monument.

🌌 Northern Lights

One of the few places in China where the aurora borealis is possible (rare but documented).

❄️ Extreme Cold

Temperatures can drop to -50°C — one of the coldest inhabited places in China.

Top Attractions

🧭 North Pole Village

Visit the 'North Pole' monument — the northernmost point of China. Take a commemorative photo.

🌊 Amur River

The border river with Russia — boat tours in summer, frozen solid in winter.

🌲 Arctic Village

A tiny settlement in the Greater Khingan Range — pristine forests and extreme wilderness.

🌌 Stargazing

With minimal light pollution, the night sky is extraordinary. Aurora possible (though rare).

Best Time to Visit Mohe

🌸 Spring (Apr-May)

Rapid thaw. May temperatures 5-15°C. Forests awaken. Rivers begin to flow again.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warm (15-25°C). Nearly 20 hours of daylight. Best time to visit. Midnight sun feeling.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Oct)

Quick transition to cold. Brilliant fall colors in the forests. September is best.

❄️ Winter (Nov-Mar)

EXTREME COLD (-30 to -50°C). For the brave. A unique extreme cold experience.

Mohe Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Winter Preparation: If visiting in winter, bring extreme cold-weather gear or buy locally. -50°C is life-threatening without proper gear.
  • Summer Visit: June-July offers nearly 24-hour daylight — a unique experience. Temperatures are comfortable (15-25°C).
  • Getting There: The overnight train from Harbin is an adventure in itself — book a sleeper cabin.
  • Border: You can see Russia across the Amur River — the border is a fascinating geopolitical experience.
