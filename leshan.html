Leshan

Home to the world's largest stone Buddha — a 71-meter colossus carved directly into a cliff face, overlooking the confluence of three rivers.

Home / Sichuan / Leshan

📍 Top Attractions

Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)

The world's largest stone Buddha at 71 meters tall. Carved over 90 years starting in 713 AD. Walk down the cliff-side path alongside this incredible statue.

Mount Emei (峨眉山)

One of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains, offering stunning sunrise views, ancient temples, and playful monkeys along the trails.

Oriental Buddha Park

A sprawling sculpture park with over 3,000 Buddhist statues and a reclining Buddha nearly 170 meters long.

🎫 Tickets & Hours

  • Giant Buddha: ¥80 (includes Wuyou Temple and Mahao Cliff Tombs)
  • Mount Emei: ¥160 (peak), ¥110 (off-peak)
  • Boat tour (Buddha from river): ¥70 — great for photos without the climb

🚄 Getting There

🚄 By High-Speed Rail

  • Chengdu to Leshan: ~50 min
  • Leshan Station to Buddha: Bus #3 or taxi

🚌 By Bus

  • Chengdu Xinnanmen Station to Leshan
  • ~2 hours
  • Departures every 30 minutes

💡 Travel Tips

  • Visit early morning to avoid crowds at the Buddha
  • River boat tour offers the best full-view photos of the Buddha
  • Combine with Mount Emei for a 2-3 day trip
  • Wear comfortable shoes — the staircase is steep
