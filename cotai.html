Cotai

Macau's 'Vegas Strip' — spectacular casino resorts, luxury shopping, and world-class entertainment.

Home / Macau / Cotai

City Overview

Cotai is Macau's modern entertainment district — a reclaimed strip of land between Taipa and Coloane that has become the 'Las Vegas of Asia.' The Cotai Strip features some of the world's largest and most spectacular casino resorts, including The Venetian (with indoor canals), Wynn Palace (with a dancing fountain), and MGM Cotai.

📍 Location

The strip between Taipa and Coloane. Connected by free casino shuttles from the ferry terminal.

🎰 Cotai Strip

Macau's 'Vegas Strip' — a concentrated area of the world's largest casino resorts.

🏨 The Venetian

A replica of Venice with indoor canals, gondolas, and Renaissance architecture — astonishing in scale.

⛲ Wynn Palace

A luxury resort with a spectacular dancing fountain and the world's largest rotating flower sculpture.

Top Attractions

🏨 The Venetian

Ride a gondola through the indoor canals — a surreal Venice experience in the heart of Asia.

⛲ Performance Lake

Wynn Palace's dancing fountain show — water, fire, and music in a spectacular free show.

🛍️ Shopping

The Cotai Strip malls house every luxury brand — duty-free shopping at its finest.

🍽️ Fine Dining

Macau has more Michelin stars per capita than any other city in Asia — many are on the Cotai Strip.

Best Time to Visit Cotai

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm. Indoor resort activities are comfortable regardless of weather.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot outside, but resorts offer air-conditioned luxury. Pool season at the resorts.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Comfortable for walking between resorts. Clear skies.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool. Perfect for exploring the resorts on foot. Fewer crowds.

Cotai Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Free Shows: Many resort shows are free — Wynn's Performance Lake, Venetian's canals, and various art installations.
  • You Don't Need to Gamble: The resorts are spectacular even without gambling — the architecture, shopping, and dining are world-class.
  • Dining: Macau has over 20 Michelin-starred restaurants — many on the Cotai Strip. Book in advance.
  • Transport: Use free casino shuttles to get between resorts — no need for taxis on the Strip.
