Best Time to Visit China: A Month-by-Month Guide

📅 From cherry blossoms to golden foliage — find your perfect travel window

📅 June 29, 2026 ⏱️ 14 min read 🏷️ Planning

📊 Quick Overview

🌸 Best Overall

April-May, September-October

💰 Cheapest

November-March (not Chinese New Year)

🎭 Most Festive

Chinese New Year, October

🌤️ Warmest

June-August

🌸 Spring (March-May)

Spring is arguably the best time to visit China. Pleasant temperatures, blooming flowers, and manageable crowds make it ideal for most travelers.

🌸 March

Weather: 5-15°C (41-59°F) in north, 10-20°C (50-68°F) in south

Crowds: Moderate, increasing toward month end

Pros: Plum blossoms, early cherry blossoms in south, fewer tourists than April-May

Cons: Can be rainy in south, still cold in north

Best for: Kunming, Yunnan, early itinerary planning

🌺 April

Weather: 10-22°C (50-72°F) nationwide, warming up nicely

Crowds: High — peak travel season begins

Pros: Cherry blossoms in Beijing, rape flower fields in Jiuzhaigou, Peach Blossom Festival in Lijiang

Cons: Hotel prices increase, book ahead

Best for: Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an

🌷 May

Weather: 15-28°C (59-82°F), warm and comfortable

Crowds: Peak spring season

Pros: Best weather overall, fewer holidays than October, long daylight hours

Cons: Getting crowded, higher prices

Best for: All destinations, especially outdoor activities

☀️ Summer (June-August)

Summer brings heat, humidity, and rain — but also school holidays and longer days. Best for specific destinations like cooler highland areas.

🌧️ June

Weather: 20-30°C (68-86°F), rainy season begins

Crowds: Moderate, student crowds before summer break

Pros: Green landscapes, fewer crowds than July-August, Dragon Boat Festival

Cons: Rainy in south (Fujian, Guangdong), hot in cities

Best for: Shangri-La, Dali, Xishuangbanna

🌡️ July

Weather: 25-35°C (77-95°F), hot and humid nationwide

Crowds: High — summer school holidays

Pros: Long days, summer festivals, highland escapes from heat

Cons: Extreme heat in cities, typhoon season in south, crowded tourist sites

Best for: Harbin (cool), tropical areas, mountain retreats

🌡️ August

Weather: 25-35°C (77-95°F), hottest month

Crowds: Very high — peak summer vacation

Pros: Beach season at Hainan, escape to mountains

Cons: Extremely crowded and hot, typhoon risks in coastal areas

Best for: Coastal Sanya, highland Yunnan, Kunming ("Spring City")

🍂 Autumn (September-November)

Autumn is the other peak season and arguably the most beautiful time to visit. Clear skies, golden foliage, and comfortable temperatures.

🍂 September

Weather: 15-28°C (59-82°F), transition from summer

Crowds: Moderate, decreasing from summer peak

Pros: Mid-Autumn Festival, shoulder season prices, cooling temperatures

Cons: Still warm in south, typhoon season continues

Best for: Beijing, Northeast (early foliage)

🍁 October

Weather: 10-22°C (50-72°F), perfect temperatures

Crowds: Very high — Golden Week holiday (Oct 1-7)

Pros: National Day holiday, peak autumn colors, comfortable everywhere

Cons: Golden Week is extremely crowded, book 3+ months ahead

Best for: Jiuzhaigou (best colors), Lijiang, Beijing

🍂 November

Weather: 5-18°C (41-64°F), cooling down

Crowds: Moderate, shoulder season begins

Pros: Fall foliage in south, fewer tourists after Golden Week, lower prices

Cons: Getting cold in north, some attractions closing for off-season

Best for: Guilin, Dali, Shanghai

❄️ Winter (December-February)

Winter offers budget-friendly travel, unique experiences, and fewer crowds — but cold temperatures in most regions.

❄️ December

Weather: 0-10°C (32-50°F) in north, 10-20°C (50-68°F) in south

Crowds: Low outside holiday period

Pros: Lowest prices, ice festivals in Harbin, clear skies

Cons: Cold in north, gray weather in some areas

Best for: Harbin Ice Festival, Hainan beach, Guangzhou

🧧 January-February (Chinese New Year)

Weather: Coldest months, varies by region

Crowds: Very high during CNY week, low before/after

Pros: Experience authentic Chinese New Year, fireworks, traditional celebrations

Cons: CNY week is chaotic, many businesses closed, transport crowded

Best for: Cultural immersion (if planned well), avoiding CNY week itself

🎊 Chinese Holidays to Avoid (or Plan Around)

🧧 Chinese New Year (Spring Festival)

When: Late January or February (23-day cycle)

Impact: Maximum disruption — book 3+ months ahead or avoid entirely

Dates 2026: January 29, 2026

🏮 National Day (Golden Week)

When: October 1-7

Impact: Massive domestic travel, extremely crowded, higher prices

Alternative: Travel late September or after October 7

🧧 Qingming Festival

When: April 4-6

Impact: 3-day holiday, increased domestic travel

Alternative: Travel late March or after April 7

🐉 Dragon Boat Festival

When: Late May or June (lunar calendar)

Impact: 3-day holiday, moderate crowds

Dates 2026: May 31, 2026

🌕 Mid-Autumn Festival

When: September or October (lunar calendar)

Impact: 3-day holiday, family gatherings

Dates 2026: September 21, 2026

💼 Labor Day

When: May 1

Impact: 3-day holiday, domestic travel surge

Dates 2026: May 1-3, 2026

💡 Final Recommendations

  • First Visit: April-May or September-October for best weather and crowds
  • Budget: November-March (avoid Chinese New Year) for lowest prices
  • Specific Interests: Cherry blossoms = April in Beijing; Autumn foliage = October in Jiuzhaigou
  • Regional Focus: Yunnan year-round, Shangri-La May-October, Hainan November-March

China's vast size means weather varies dramatically by region. No single "perfect" time exists for the entire country. The key is matching your timing to your destinations and being prepared for regional weather variations.
