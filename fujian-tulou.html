Fujian Tulou

UNESCO-listed earthen fortresses — 800-year-old communal dwellings found nowhere else on Earth.

Home / Fujian Province / Fujian Tulou

City Overview

The Fujian Tulou are massive earthen roundhouses and square houses built between the 12th and 20th centuries. These UNESCO-listed structures housed entire clans — up to 800 people living in a single circular building. They are found only in the mountainous region of western Fujian.

📍 Location

Western Fujian, in the mountains of Yongding and Nanjing counties.

🏯 Round Houses

Massive circular earthen buildings — some 800 years old, housing 50-80 families.

🏘️ Communal Living

A unique social experiment — an entire clan lives behind one fortified wall.

🌿 Mountain Setting

Set among tea gardens, bamboo forests, and terraced rice paddies.

Top Attractions

🏯 Yongding Tulou Cluster

The most famous cluster — the 'King of Tulou' (承启楼) has 4 rings and 400 rooms.

🏛️ Nanjing Tulou Cluster

The 'Four Dishes and One Soup' arrangement — five tulou in a unique landscape setting.

🏘️ Tianluokeng Tulou

The iconic 'four dishes and one soup' — the most photographed tulou arrangement.

🌾 Village Life

Watch daily life inside a tulou — cooking, weaving, and communal activities continue as for centuries.

Best Time to Visit Fujian Tulou

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Pleasant. Green landscapes. Rice paddies being planted. Few tourists.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot. Lush scenery. Rice terraces at their greenest. Harvest festivals possible.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Golden rice harvest. Clear skies. Photography at its best.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool. Quiet. Chinese New Year celebrations inside the tulou are spectacular.

Fujian Tulou Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Stay Overnight: Sleep inside a tulou guesthouse — the experience of living in an 800-year-old fortress is unforgettable.
  • Yongding vs Nanjing: Yongding has the biggest tulou; Nanjing has the most photogenic settings. Visit both if possible.
  • Transport: Hire a car — the clusters are scattered across mountain roads with limited public transport.
  • Respect: People still live inside these tulou — ask before photographing residents. Buy local tea as thanks.
