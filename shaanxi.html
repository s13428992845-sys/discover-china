Shaanxi Province

Known as the "Cradle of Chinese Civilization" — walk with 2,000-year-old warriors, cycle atop ancient city walls, and taste the noodles that started a thousand imitations.

Home / Provinces / Shaanxi

Province Overview

Shaanxi is a province in Central China, home to Xi'an — one of China's Four Great Ancient Capitals. As the birthplace of Chinese civilization, it holds some of the country's most significant historical treasures, including the world-famous Terracotta Warriors and the ancient city walls of Xi'an.

📍 Location

Central China, connecting east and west. Home to Xi'an, one of China's Four Great Ancient Capitals.

👥 Population

~39.5 million. Xi'an metro area alone has 13 million people.

🌤 Climate

Temperate continental. Hot summers, cold winters. Spring and autumn are ideal travel seasons.

⛰ Terrain

Loess Plateau in north, Qinling Mountains in south, Guanzhong Plain in center.

🍜 Cuisine

Famous for biangbiang noodles, roujiamo (Chinese hamburger), and dumpling banquets.

✈️ Major Airport

Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY), 2 hours by air from most major Chinese cities.

Why Visit Shaanxi?

🏺 Terracotta Warriors

The world-famous 8,000-strong clay army guarding Emperor Qin's tomb for over 2,200 years. A UNESCO World Heritage Site.

🏯 Ancient City Wall

Xi'an's 600-year-old city wall, the best-preserved in China. Rent a bike and cycle the 13.7km loop.

🥙 Muslim Quarter

A vibrant food street dating back to the Silk Road era. Try roujiamo, yangrou paomo, and persimmon cakes.

🏔️ Mount Hua

One of China's Five Sacred Mountains. Famous for its vertigo-inducing plank walk along a sheer cliff face.

🗼 Big Wild Goose Pagoda

A 7th-century Buddhist pagoda built to house sutras brought from India by the monk Xuanzang.

🕳️ Hanyang Tombs

The spectacular underground glass-museum showing miniature Han Dynasty figurines and animals.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (March - May)

Perfect weather, blooming flowers. Ideal for hiking Mount Hua.

☀️ Summer (June - August)

Hot but lively. Great for evening food tours in Muslim Quarter.

🍂 Autumn (September - November)

Clear skies, comfortable temperatures. Best photography season.

❄️ Winter (December - February)

Cold but fewer crowds. Budget-friendly with indoor cultural sites.

Getting Around Shaanxi

✈️ By Air

  • Major Airport: Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY) — a major hub with flights across China and some international routes
  • Domestic Flights: 2 hours from most major Chinese cities
  • Budget Flights: Often available at competitive prices

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Connects Xi'an to Beijing (4.5hrs), Shanghai (6hrs), Chengdu (3.5hrs)
  • Metro: Xi'an Metro covers major city areas and tourist sites
  • Local Trains: Affordable options for traveling within Shaanxi

🚌 By Bus

  • Tourist Shuttles: City buses and tourist shuttles connect major attractions
  • Terracotta Warriors Bus: Bus #306 from Xi'an, convenient and affordable
  • Long-Distance Buses: To Mount Hua and other regional destinations

🚗 Car / Metro

  • Metro: Xi'an Metro is the best way to get around the city center
  • Rent a Car: For Mount Hua or Hanzhong day trips
  • Ride Hailing: Didi works well in Xi'an and major cities

Shaanxi Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Book Terracotta Warriors Tickets Online: Book in advance — daily visitor limits apply.
  • City Wall Bike Rental: Costs ~45 CNY for 2 hours — bring sunscreen and water.
  • Muslim Quarter is Halal: Respect dietary customs, don't bring pork or alcohol.
  • Mount Hua Plank Walk: Requires a separate fee (~30 CNY) and a signed waiver.
  • Visa-Free Transit: Xi'an has a 144-hour visa-free transit policy for many nationalities.
  • Download Amap or Baidu Maps: Google Maps doesn't work well in China.
  • Tang Dynasty Music and Dance Show: A must-see evening performance in Xi'an.
  • Biangbiang Noodles are Huge: One bowl is a full meal. Come hungry!
