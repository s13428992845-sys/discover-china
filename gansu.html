Gansu Province

Silk Road treasures — Dunhuang's Mogao Caves, Zhangye's rainbow mountains, and the western end of the Great Wall at Jiayuguan.

Home / Provinces / Gansu Province

Province Overview

Silk Road treasures — Dunhuang's Mogao Caves, Zhangye's rainbow mountains, and the western end of the Great Wall at Jiayuguan.

📍 Location

Northwest China, a long narrow corridor connecting Central China to the Silk Road. Borders Gansu runs from southeast to northwest for 1,600km.

👥 Population

Approximately 25 million. Home to Han, Hui, Tibetan, Dongxiang, and Yugur ethnic groups.

🌡️ Climate

Extremely varied — subtropical southeast to arid desert northwest. Annual rainfall ranges from 800mm to 30mm.

🏔️ Terrain

Incredible diversity: Gobi Desert, Qilian Mountains, Loess Plateau, and the Hexi Corridor oasis strip.

🍜 Cuisine

Lanzhou beef noodles (China's most famous noodle soup), Hand-pulled mutton, Dunhuang local dishes, and Silk Road fusion cuisine.

✈️ Major Airport

Lanzhou Zhongchuan (LHW) — provincial hub. Dunhuang (DNH) for Mogao Caves visitors.

Why Visit Gansu Province?

🎨 Mogao Caves

1,600-year-old Buddhist art treasury with 492 caves, 45,000m² of murals, and 2,400 painted sculptures — a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

🌈 Zhangye Danxia

The 'Rainbow Mountains' — surreal striped rock formations in vivid reds, yellows, and oranges. Best at sunset.

🏯 Jiayuguan Pass

The western terminus of the Great Wall — a massive fortified citadel guarding the edge of the Gobi Desert since 1372.

🐫 Mingsha Sand Dunes

Singing sand dunes near Dunhuang — ride camels at sunset across the edge of the Taklamakan Desert.

🏛️ Labrang Monastery

One of six major Gelug monasteries of Tibetan Buddhism, with the world's longest prayer wheel corridor.

🌊 Crescent Lake

A miraculous crescent-shaped oasis surrounded by sand dunes near Dunhuang — an ancient Silk Road landmark.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming up but still dry. Desert landscapes are pleasant. Zhangye Danxia starts to show vivid colors.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Peak season. Hot in the desert but perfect for Dunhuang and Zhangye. Book Mogao Caves well in advance.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Cool, dry, clear skies. Golden poplar forests and perfect hiking weather.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Very cold, especially in the northwest. Fewer crowds and lower prices, but some attractions have reduced hours.

Getting Around Gansu Province

✈️ By Air

  • Airport: Lanzhou is the main hub with flights to most major Chinese cities. Dunhuang has seasonal flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Xi'an.

🚄 By Train

  • Railway: Lanzhou is a major railway junction. High-speed rail connects to Xi'an (3h), Urumqi (12h). The ancient Silk Road route.

🚌 By Bus

  • Bus: Long-distance buses connect cities within Gansu. Comfortable sleeper buses to Dunhuang from Lanzhou (14h).

🚗 By Car

  • Driving: Self-driving the Hexi Corridor is epic. Well-maintained highways connect Lanzhou to Dunhuang via Zhangye and Jiayuguan.

Gansu Province Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Book Mogao Caves Early: Limited to 6,000 visitors/day in peak season. Book 2-4 weeks ahead on the official website.
  • Try Lanzhou Noodles: Eat beef noodles for breakfast like locals — the soup is best before 9AM when it's freshest.
  • Zhangye at Sunset: The Rainbow Mountains look most spectacular 1-2 hours before sunset when colors intensify.
  • Camel Trekking: Negotiate prices before riding camels at Mingsha dunes. Sunset rides are most photogenic.
  • Altitude Note: Lanzhou is at 1,500m — mild altitude effects possible. Acclimatize before heading to higher areas.
  • Bring Water: The northwest is extremely dry. Carry water and moisturizer at all times.
  • Silk Road Route: Plan 5-7 days for the classic Lanzhou → Zhangye → Jiayuguan → Dunhuang route.
  • Muslim Culture: Gansu has a significant Hui Muslim population. Try halal restaurants for excellent mutton dishes.
