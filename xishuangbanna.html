Xishuangbanna

Tropical paradise where Chinese, Thai, and Laotian cultures meet. Ancient Dai traditions, lush rainforests, exotic wildlife, and the mighty Mekong River await you.

Home / Yunnan / Xishuangbanna

City Overview

📍 Location

Southernmost prefecture of Yunnan, bordering Laos and Myanmar. Gateway to Southeast Asia. Subtropical climate with year-round warmth. Population ~1.1 million.

🎭 Character

Dai ethnic minority culture, Theravada Buddhism, water splashing festival. Tropical rainforests, rare wildlife (Asian elephants, peacocks), fruit plantations, and ancient temples. Climate similar to Northern Thailand.

🌡️ Climate

Hot year-round (15-28°C average). Best: October-April (dry season). Rainy season May-September (short afternoon showers). Pack light, breathable clothing.

🛂 Border Access

Close to Laos and Myanmar borders. Mohan border crossing to Laos (Xishuangbanna-Mengla). Popular starting point for overland travel through Southeast Asia.

💡 Why Visit Xishuangbanna?

  • Tropical Climate: Year-round warmth, lush greenery, exotic fruits year-round
  • Dai Culture: Living culture — Buddhism, traditional houses, water splashing festival
  • Wildlife: Asian elephants, golden monkeys, exotic birds in nature reserves
  • Cuisine: Spicy, sour, and tropical — mango sticky rice, herbs, tropical fruits
  • Rainforests: Ancient rainforests, sacred groves, nature reserves

Top Attractions

Wild Elephant Valley

Wild Elephant Valley (野象谷)

Nature reserve and national park protecting China's remaining wild Asian elephants. Home to ~150 wild elephants along with golden monkeys, peacocks, and tropical birds. Take the canopy walkway (elevated forest path) for safe wildlife viewing. Elephant training shows and feeding times daily. Part of Xishuangbanna UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

💰 Ticket: ¥120
🕐 Open: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Distance: ~45 km, 1 hour
Nature Wildlife Family
Manting Park

Manting Park (曼听公园)

"The Crown Jewel of Xishuangbanna" — oldest and most beautiful of Xishuangbanna's parks. Features ancient temples, lotus lakes, peacocks, and traditional Dai architecture. Site of the Royal Park of the Dai Kingdom. Don't miss the evening Song of the Dai People (傣族民族歌舞) — traditional dance and music performance under the stars.

💰 Ticket: ¥75 (performance extra)
🕐 Open: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Location: Jinghong City Center
Gardens Culture Peacocks
Tropical Botanical Garden

Xishuangbanna Tropical Botanical Garden (中科院植物园)

China's largest tropical botanical garden — 900+ hectares, 13,000+ plant species. Divided into sections: medicinal plants, palm trees, orchids, tropical rainforest, and rare plant protection. The "King of Plants" — see the giant Raffaeldia palm. Rent a bike (¥30) or take the electric train to cover more ground.

💰 Ticket: ¥80
🕐 Open: 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Distance: ~75 km, 1.5-2 hours
Nature Plants Science
Dai Culture Village

Dai Culture Village (傣族文化园)

Cultural immersion into Dai ethnic minority traditions. Traditional Dai houses (木楼 — raised wooden houses with thatched roofs), silver workshops, traditional medicine demonstrations, and live performances. Try your hand at Dai weaving, dance with locals, and learn about Buddhism. Beautiful location near ancient banyan trees.

💰 Ticket: ¥60
🕐 Open: 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM
📍 Distance: ~20 km, 30 min
Culture Ethnic Workshops
Mekong River Cruise

Mekong River Cruise

The Mekong (澜沧江) flows through Xishuangbanna. Take a boat tour to experience life along the river — riverside villages, rice paddies, fishermen. Sunset cruises (¥50-80) offer golden light and views of the city. Longer trips (2-3 days) go to the border with Laos. Check if cruise boat crosses border — can combine with Laos visa-free transit.

💰 Cost: ¥50-200 (varies by duration)
🕐 Duration: 1-3 hours to multi-day
📍 Location: Multiple points in Jinghong

Local Food & Restaurants

Dai cuisine — spicy, sour, and tropical with Thai and Laotian influences

🥭

Mango Sticky Rice (Mango Muang)

Sweet mango served over coconut sticky rice. Classic Thai-Dai dessert, perfect breakfast or snack. Available at markets and casual restaurants. "Thai Restaurant" near the bus station makes an excellent version.

🍲

Dai Hotpot (Dai Huo Guo)

Spicy, sour hot pot with local vegetables, herbs, and freshwater fish. Different from other Yunnan hot pot — uses lots of sour and fresh herbs. Green papaya (hairy papaya), lemon grass, and chilies give it distinctive flavor.

🐔

Dai Roasted Chicken (Dai Ka Yao)

Whole chicken marinated in herbs and spices, roasted over charcoal. Crispy skin, tender meat, served with dipping sauces and fresh vegetables. Traditional Dai feast dish. Found at traditional restaurants and during festivals.

🌿

Fresh Herb Salads (Larb & Yum)

Mix of fresh herbs, spices, chilies, and sometimes meat. Spicy, tangy, refreshing. Similar to Thai dishes — influence from neighboring Southeast Asia. Try at Dai restaurants for authentic flavors.

🍵

Tropical Fruits

Year-round tropical fruits — mango, papaya, dragon fruit, durian, rambutan, mangosteen. Try at morning markets. Fresh fruit juices and smoothies everywhere (¥10-20). Don't miss the "Night Market" fruit stalls — peak freshness.

🍽️ Where to Eat

  • Night Market (夜市): Open 7 PM-1 AM. Street food, seafood, fruit
  • Dai Restaurants: Look for "傣味" signs in Chinese. Traditional dishes
  • Market Areas: Jinghong morning market, Xishuangbanna Market — local flavors
  • Riverside Dining: Cafes along the Mekong, western-style options available

Transportation Guide

✈️ Getting to Xishuangbanna

  • Xishuangbanna Gasa Airport (JHG): Direct flights from Kunming, Bangkok, Chiang Mai. Metro line 6 from Kunming + bus to airport.
  • Train from Kunming: New high-speed line (Xishuangbanna Station). 3-4 hours.
  • Bus from Kunming: Xiaguan Bus Station → Jinghong (¥140, 12-14 hours).
  • From Laos: Bus from Luang Prabang (10 hours) or Vientiane (15 hours).

🛵 Getting Around

  • Moto-Taxi / Tuk-Tuk: Most common. ¥10-20 within city. Bargaining accepted.
  • Didi: Available but limited coverage outside Jinghong.
  • Bus: Regular buses to attractions (¥5-20). Long distance buses to other cities.
  • Bicycle/E-bike: Great for Jinghong and nearby areas. ¥50-100/day.

🚌 Day Trips

  • Wild Elephant Valley: Bus from Jinghong (¥30, 1 hour) or organized tour (¥200-400)
  • Manting Park: Central Jinghong — walk or short taxi ride
  • Botanical Garden: Bus from central bus station (¥15, 1.5 hours)
  • Dai Villages: Multiple nearby villages. Cycle or short moto-taxi rides

🛂 Border Crossings

  • Mohan Border: To Laos (Boten). Can arrange Laos visa on arrival.
  • Ruili Border: To Myanmar (Muse). More complex, requires Myanmar visa.
  • Visa-Free Transit: Xishuangbanna-Laos 144-hour visa-free transit available.
  • Advance Planning: Check current visa requirements before traveling to borders.
