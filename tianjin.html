Tianjin

A fascinating blend of East and West — colonial architecture, the iconic Tianjin Eye, delicious street food, and a gateway between Beijing and the Bohai Sea.

Home / Provinces / Tianjin

Province Overview

Tianjin, China's fourth-largest city, is a fascinating port city with a unique blend of Chinese and European influences. Once home to nine foreign concessions, the city features remarkable colonial architecture alongside modern marvels like the Tianjin Eye ferris wheel. Just 30 minutes from Beijing by high-speed train, it makes an excellent day trip or destination.

📍 Location

Northern China, on the Bohai Sea coast. 120km southeast of Beijing. Major port city.

👥 Population

Approximately 14 million. One of China's four direct-controlled municipalities.

🌡️ Climate

Temperate monsoon. Cold dry winters, hot summers. Best visited April-October.

🏔️ Terrain

Flat coastal plain along the Bohai Sea. Hai River runs through the city center.

🍜 Cuisine

Famous for goubuli baozi (steamed buns), ear hole fried cake, mahua (fried dough twist), and Jianbing Guozi.

✈️ Major Airport

Tianjin Binhai International Airport (TSN) — growing international connections.

Why Visit Tianjin?

🎡 Tianjin Eye

The world's only ferris wheel built on a bridge — 120m tall, offering stunning views of the Hai River and city.

🏛️ Five Great Avenues

Area with 2,000+ European-style buildings from the colonial era. Called 'Museum of World Architecture'.

🇮🇹 Italian Quarter

The largest Italian-style town outside Italy. Beautiful piazza, restaurants, and European atmosphere.

🏮 Ancient Culture Street

Traditional Chinese street with folk art, crafts, Tianhou Temple, and famous local snacks.

🏔️ Panshan Mountain

One of northern China's most scenic mountains, 1.5 hours from the city. Ancient temples and natural beauty.

🌊 Binhai New Area

Futuristic district with the iconic Binhai Library 'The Eye' and modern architecture.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (March - May)

Warming weather, occasional sandstorms in early spring. Pleasant for walking tours.

☀️ Summer (June - August)

Hot and humid, 30°C+. River cruises and evening activities are popular.

🍂 Autumn (September - November)

Best season. Cool, clear skies, comfortable temperatures for exploring.

❄️ Winter (December - February)

Cold and dry, -5 to 3°C. Fewer tourists. Good for hot pot and indoor attractions.

Getting Around Tianjin

✈️ By Air

  • Tianjin Binhai (TSN): International airport with connections to major Asian cities
  • To Beijing: Can fly into Beijing and take HSR to Tianjin in 30 minutes
  • Budget Airlines: Often cheaper to fly into Tianjin than Beijing for northern China travel

🚄 By Train

  • Beijing-Tianjin HSR: Only 30 minutes from Beijing South Station. Trains every 5 minutes
  • Intercity: Connected to major cities in northern China
  • Metro: Extensive subway system connecting all major areas

🚇 By Metro

  • Network: Multiple lines covering major attractions and districts
  • Hai River Line: Connects historic center to Binhai New Area
  • Cost: 2-6 CNY per trip. Very affordable

🚗 Car Rental

  • Self-Drive: Expressways connect to Beijing and surrounding areas
  • To Panshan: 1.5 hours by car, good for day trips
  • Parking: Can be challenging in the old city area

Tianjin Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Day Trip from Beijing: Just 30 min by HSR. Perfect for a day trip — start early to see everything.
  • Jianbing Guozi: Tianjin's famous breakfast crepe. Try the authentic version at street stalls — much better than other cities.
  • Eye on the Bridge: Book Tianjin Eye tickets in advance, especially on weekends. Sunset rides are magical.
  • Colonial Architecture: Rent a bike to explore the Five Great Avenues area — much more efficient than walking.
  • Binhai Library: The 'Eye' library is stunning but photography restrictions may apply. Check ahead.
  • Cross-Talk: Tianjin is the birthplace of Chinese cross-talk (xiangsheng). Catch a live show if possible.
  • Snack Street: Try Goubuli baozi (even if locals say it's overrated), ear hole fried cake, and mahua.
  • Porcelain House: A building completely covered in ancient porcelain pieces — a unique and dazzling attraction.
