Inner Mongolia

Known as the "Land of Endless Blue Sky" — ride horses across vast grasslands, sleep in traditional yurts under the Milky Way, and experience the nomadic spirit of China's northern frontier.

Home / Provinces / Inner Mongolia

Province Overview

Inner Mongolia is an autonomous region stretching across northern China, spanning 2,400km from east to west. It borders Russia and Mongolia, offering vast grasslands, the Gobi Desert, and one of China's most distinctive nomadic cultures. China's third-largest province-level region is a destination like no other.

📍 Location

Northern China, spanning 2,400km from east to west. Borders Russia and Mongolia. China's third-largest province-level region.

👥 Population

~24 million. Mongolian ethnic group is the largest minority, with Han Chinese as majority.

🌤 Climate

Continental. Short warm summers, extremely cold winters. Best visited June-September.

⛰ Terrain

Vast grasslands (Hulunbuir, Xilingol), Gobi Desert in the west, Greater Khingan Mountains in the east.

🍜 Cuisine

Roast lamb, milk tea, hand-pulled mutton, dairy products. Hearty nomadic cuisine.

✈️ Major Airport

Hohhot Baita International Airport (HET). Also Baotou (BAV) and Hulunbuir (HLD).

Why Visit Inner Mongolia?

🌿 Hulunbuir Grassland

One of the world's finest grasslands. Endless green waves stretching to the horizon — the "purest grassland on Earth."

🏕 Nomadic Experience

Stay in traditional Mongolian yurts, ride horses, milk cows, and experience the authentic nomadic lifestyle.

🏜️ Gobi Desert

China's portion of the legendary Gobi. Camel rides, singing sand dunes, and fossil-rich badlands.

🏆 Naadam Festival

The "Three Games of Men" — wrestling, horse racing, and archery. Held every July, a spectacular cultural event.

🎵 Singing Sand Bay

Resonant Sand Dunes (Xiangshawan) produce musical sounds when you slide down them. A surreal desert experience.

🌌 Star Gazing

Zero light pollution on the grasslands. Some of the best stargazing in all of Asia.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar - May)

Warming up but still windy. Grass starting to green. Fewer tourists.

☀️ Summer (Jun - Aug)

Peak season! Green grasslands, Naadam Festival, perfect weather (20-25°C).

🍂 Autumn (Sep - Oct)

Golden grasslands, crisp air. Excellent photography. Cooling down fast.

❄️ Winter (Nov - Feb)

Extremely cold (-30°C). For the brave: ice festivals and snow grasslands.

Getting Around Inner Mongolia

✈️ By Air

  • Major Airport: Hohhot (HET) and Hulunbuir (HLD) — main gateways
  • Regional Airports: Baotou (BAV) for western Inner Mongolia
  • Budget Flights: Limited direct international flights — connect via Beijing

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Beijing to Hohhot ~2.5 hours
  • Overnight Trains: Available to Hulunbuir and other major cities
  • Scenic Routes: Train journeys offer glimpses of the vast landscape

🚌 By Bus

  • Long-Distance Buses: Connect major towns and cities
  • Routes: Can be very long (8-12 hours between cities)
  • Local Buses: To smaller towns and grassland areas

🚗 Car / 4WD

  • Essential: For grassland exploration, a car or 4WD is essential
  • Hire Local Driver: Highly recommended — know the terrain well
  • Organized Tours: Many packages available from Hohhot

Inner Mongolia Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Book Ahead: Summer (Jul-Aug) is peak season — book yurt accommodations and grassland tours in advance.
  • Layer Up: Temperature can drop 15°C between day and night on the grassland. Bring layers!
  • Respect Customs: Don't step on the yurt threshold, accept tea with your right hand.
  • Horse Riding: Costs ~100-200 CNY — always agree on price and duration before mounting.
  • Long Distances: The distance between attractions is enormous — plan for long driving days.
  • Sun Protection: Bring sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat — the grassland sun is intense with no shade.
  • Try Local Food: Try the local milk tea (salty, not sweet!) and roast lamb — it's an essential experience.
  • Offline Maps: Cell signal is weak on the grasslands — download offline maps and entertainment beforehand.
