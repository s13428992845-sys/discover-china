Sanya

China's premier tropical beach resort city with pristine bays, luxury resorts, and the iconic Nanshan Temple.

Home / Hainan Province / Sanya

City Overview

Sanya is China's answer to Hawaii — a tropical paradise on Hainan's southern tip. With four major bays offering different experiences, world-class resorts, and year-round warm weather, it's the country's most popular beach destination. The city also features the famous 108-meter Guanyin statue and vibrant night markets.

📍 Location

Southern tip of Hainan Island. China's most southern city.

👥 Population

Approximately 1 million permanent, millions more tourists annually.

🌡️ Climate

Tropical, 24-32°C year-round. Best: Nov-Mar (dry and comfortable).

🏛️ Character

International resort city with luxury hotels, beaches, and tropical atmosphere.

Top Attractions

🏖️ Yalong Bay

The finest beach in Sanya — 7km of white sand, clear water, and luxury resorts lining the coast.

🙏 Nanshan Temple

Home to the 108m Guanyin statue rising from the sea. One of the tallest statues in the world.

🏝️ Wuzhizhou Island

China's top diving and snorkeling destination with crystal waters and vibrant coral reefs.

🌅 Luhuitou

'Deer Looking Back' hilltop park offering spectacular sunset views over the entire bay.

Best Time to Visit Sanya

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Perfect beach weather. Water warming up. Less crowded before summer.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot but great for water sports. Some typhoon risk. Lower hotel rates.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Water still warm, crowds thinning. Excellent value season.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Peak season. Perfect 25°C weather. Book well in advance.

Sanya Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Beach Choice: Yalong Bay for luxury, Dadonghai for budget, Sanya Bay for sunsets, Haitang Bay for seclusion.
  • Duty-Free Mall: Visit the world's largest duty-free complex in Haitang Bay. Bring passport and flight info.
  • Seafood Market: Buy fresh seafood at the First Market and have nearby restaurants cook it for you.
  • Sunscreen: Bring reef-safe sunscreen if snorkeling. Regular sunscreen damages coral.
