How to Use WeChat Pay & Alipay in China: Complete 2026 Guide for Foreigners

💳 Go cash-free in China — pay like a local using your phone

💡 Why You Can't Ignore Mobile Payment in China

Here's the reality: cash is dying in China. In major cities, even small vendors at wet markets, street food stalls, and路边水果摊 (roadside fruit stands) all expect WeChat Pay or Alipay. Handing over a 100-yuan note at a ¥8 breakfast stall will get you a look of bewilderment — or worse, they'll charge you more for the inconvenience.

This isn't just a big-city thing. Mobile payment penetration in China exceeds 87% of all transactions. Even in Shangri-La, Lijiang, and other tourist towns in Yunnan, vendors will point you to their QR code before reaching for a cash box.

The good news: setting up mobile payment as a foreign tourist has become dramatically easier since 2019. Both WeChat and Alipay now support international credit and debit cards directly — no Chinese bank account needed.

📱 Option 1: WeChat Pay (Setup Guide)

WeChat (known as Weixin in Chinese) is China's dominant super-app — messaging, social media, ride-hailing, food delivery, and payments all in one. If you're connecting with Chinese friends, colleagues, or hosts, WeChat is essential beyond just payments.

Step 1: Download WeChat

Download WeChat from the App Store or Google Play before you arrive in China (it's blocked in Chinese app stores for international phone numbers). Create an account using your international phone number (+1, +44, etc.).

Step 2: Upgrade to WeChat Pay

Open WeChat → Go to Me → Services (may appear as "Wallet" in some versions) → Tap "Pay" → Follow the prompts to add a card.

You'll need to verify your identity with:

  • Your passport number
  • Passport photo (the app will guide you through this)
  • Your international phone number (must match the one registered with WeChat)

Step 3: Link Your Card

Supported cards include Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and Discover. The process:

  • Select your card type (Visa/Mastercard/etc.)
  • Enter your card number and expiry date
  • Confirm via SMS verification from your bank
  • Wait 3-5 days for identity verification to complete

⏰ Important: Verification Takes Time

Identity verification for WeChat Pay can take 3-5 business days. Don't wait until you land in China to start this process. Begin your registration at least a week before departure.

WeChat Pay: Spending Limits

💰 Daily Limits (Foreign Cards)

Single transaction: ¥3,000 (~$410)

Annual cumulative: ¥50,000 (~$6,850)

Per merchant per day: ¥5,000

💱 Currency & Fees

Automatic conversion to your card's currency

Foreign transaction fee: Typically 1.5-2% from your bank

No WeChat fees for adding money

How to Pay with WeChat Pay

When you're ready to pay, simply open WeChat → Services → Payments and show the QR code. The vendor scans it, you enter the amount on your phone, confirm with your PIN, and done. It takes about 5 seconds once you're used to it.

💳 Option 2: Alipay Tour Pass (Easier for Tourists)

Alipay Tour Pass is Alipay's dedicated solution for international visitors — and for most tourists, it's the easier option. It works like a prepaid card, and the setup is faster than WeChat Pay.

What is Tour Pass?

Tour Pass is a prepaid, reloadable digital wallet within the Alipay app specifically designed for non-Chinese nationals. You load it with a fixed amount of Chinese Yuan before your trip, then use it like any Alipay account. When it's empty, you reload.

Step 1: Download Alipay

Download Alipay from the App Store or Google Play before arrival. You'll need to set up an Alipay account first using your international phone number.

Step 2: Open Tour Pass

In the Alipay home screen, look for "Tour PASS" or "付惠卡" (Fu Hui Ka) in English mode. It's specifically designed for foreign visitors.

Step 3: Enter Your Details

  • Passport number and photo
  • Name (as it appears on your passport)
  • Nationality
  • International phone number for verification

Step 4: Load Money

Add funds using your Visa, Mastercard, or JCB card. You can load:

  • Minimum: ¥50 (~$7)
  • Maximum: ¥2,000 per load (multiple loads allowed)
  • Total balance cap: ¥6,000

✅ Why Tour Pass is Often Better

  • Setup takes minutes, not days
  • No foreign transaction fees from Alipay
  • Works immediately upon loading
  • Accepted everywhere WeChat Pay is (nearly everywhere)
  • Unused balance can be refunded

📍 Where Mobile Payment Works in China

Short answer: everywhere. But here's a practical breakdown:

✅ Always Works

  • Supermarkets & convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, Walmart)
  • All restaurants (from Michelin-star to street stalls)
  • Metro/subway (scan QR at gate)
  • Didi ride-hailing
  • Taobao and online shopping
  • Hotels and hostels
  • Tourist attractions (most accept both)
  • Street vendors and markets

⚠️ Sometimes Tricky

  • Very small rural shops (have cash backup)
  • High-end Western restaurants (some prefer card)
  • Some hotels requiring foreign registration
  • Tax refunds (often card-based anyway)

💵 When You Still Need Cash

Despite the cashless revolution, there are a few situations where you'll want physical RMB:

  • Tips — Not expected in China, but appreciated. Cash only.
  • Red envelopes (红包) — Gift money at weddings or Chinese New Year, cash in red envelopes
  • Very remote areas — Mountain villages in Yunnan or Sichuan may not have QR codes
  • Buddhist temple donations — Some prefer cash
  • Emergency backup — Always have ¥200-500 as emergency reserve

Where to get cash:

  • ATM at airport — Best first stop (Bank of China, ICBC ATMs)
  • Hotel ATMs — Usually accept international cards
  • Bank branches — Bring your passport for identification
  • Currency exchange counters — Available at airports and major banks

❓ WeChat Pay vs. Alipay: Which Should You Use?

📱 WeChat Pay

Best for: Communication + payments combined, connecting with Chinese people

Pros: All-in-one super app, already chatting with locals

Cons: Slower verification, daily limit restrictions

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ (Moderate)

💳 Alipay Tour Pass

Best for: Pure payments, quick setup, no-fuss tourists

Pros: Fast setup, no fees, simple interface

Cons: Separate from messaging app

Difficulty: ⭐ (Easy)

My recommendation: Set up both. Alipay Tour Pass for fast, easy payments. WeChat Pay for communicating with Chinese contacts and using the Didi app. Both accepted at virtually the same places.

🔧 Troubleshooting Common Problems

"My card was declined"

Notify your bank before traveling that you'll be using your card in China — this prevents fraud blocks. Also check that your card has international transaction enabled.

"WeChat Pay verification is stuck"

Try using a VPN during registration. The WeChat servers sometimes have issues processing foreign passport data without it. If still stuck after 5 days, visit a WeChat customer service center in a major city.

"I ran out of balance on Tour Pass"

Simply open Alipay → Tour Pass → Reload. Takes 30 seconds with your card.

"Can I get a refund of unused balance?"

Yes, for Alipay Tour Pass — contact Alipay customer service or visit an Alipay partner bank. For WeChat Pay, balance can be used for future trips or transferred back to your card (takes 5-7 business days).

"Do I need a Chinese phone number?"

No — both WeChat Pay and Alipay Tour Pass work with international phone numbers. However, having a Chinese SIM or eSIM makes the experience smoother (especially for receiving verification codes).

🗺️ WeChat & Alipay in Different Parts of China

Whether you're exploring the karst peaks of Guangxi, hiking in Yunnan Province, or wandering the neon streets of Shanghai, mobile payment works the same everywhere.

From the night markets of Kunming to the tea terraces of Dali, both payment systems are universally accepted. The only places to carry backup cash are genuinely remote villages off the tourist trail.

📋 Quick Setup Checklist

  • ⬜ Download WeChat and Alipay before departure
  • ⬜ Set up Alipay Tour Pass (takes 5 minutes)
  • ⬜ Start WeChat Pay verification 1 week early
  • ⬜ Notify your bank of China travel
  • ⬜ Download a VPN (WeChat verification may need it)
  • ⬜ Bring ¥500 cash for emergencies and airport arrival
  • ⬜ Screenshot hotel address in Chinese characters
  • ⬜ Download Didi (works with both payment methods)
