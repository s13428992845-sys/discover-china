China Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before your trip to China

📋 Visa & Entry Requirements

Do You Need a Visa?

Most foreign visitors need a visa to enter China. The most common type for tourists is the L visa (tourist visa). However, China offers several visa-free transit options:

  • 24-Hour Visa-Free Transit: Available at most international airports for any nationality
  • 72/144-Hour Visa-Free Transit: Available for citizens of 54 countries in designated cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Chongqing, Xi'an, and more). You must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third country/region.
  • Hainan 30-Day Visa-Free: Citizens of 59 countries can visit Hainan province visa-free for up to 30 days through a travel agency
  • Visa-Free for Certain Countries: As of 2025, citizens of France, Germany, Italy, Netherlands, Spain, Malaysia, and several others can enter China visa-free for up to 15 days

Tip: Apply for your visa at least 1-2 months before travel. You'll need a passport valid for 6+ months, a completed application form, a photo, and proof of accommodation.

💳 Mobile Payments (Essential!)

WeChat Pay & Alipay — China Goes Cashless

China is essentially a cashless society. WeChat Pay (微信支付) and Alipay (支付宝) are accepted everywhere — from luxury malls to street food stalls.

  • Download WeChat before your trip. In WeChat → Me → Services → Wallet → Add international card (Visa/Mastercard supported since 2023)
  • Download Alipay. Tap "Add Bank Card" and link your international card directly
  • Both apps now support linking Visa, Mastercard, JCB, and Diners Club cards
  • Transaction limits: up to ¥50,000 per transaction for foreign-linked accounts

⚠️ Important: Some small vendors only accept QR code payments. Always have both WeChat Pay and Alipay set up before arriving. Cash is rarely used and some vendors may not have change.

🚄 Getting Around

High-Speed Rail

China has the world's largest high-speed rail network (45,000+ km). Trains reach speeds of 350 km/h.

  • Trip.com — English interface, supports international cards
  • 12306.cn — Official railway app (now has English version)
  • Book early for popular routes, especially during holidays

Ride-Hailing & Metro

  • Didi (滴滴) — China's Uber. Available in English or through Alipay/WeChat mini-programs
  • Metro/Subway — Available in 40+ cities. Use WeChat/Alipay QR codes at the gate
  • Apple Maps — Works well in China with English support
  • Baidu Maps / Amap (高德) — Most accurate, but limited English

Flights

For long distances, domestic flights are affordable. Major airlines: Air China, China Eastern, China Southern. Book through Trip.com. Arrive 2 hours early for domestic flights.

📱 Internet & Connectivity

  • China has its own internet ecosystem. Google, Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube, and many Western sites are blocked
  • Buy a local SIM card at the airport (China Mobile, China Unicom, China Telecom). Bring your passport. Tourist SIMs cost ¥50-200
  • eSIM — If your phone supports it, buy before arrival (options from Airalo, Holafly)
  • Essential apps: WeChat (communication + payments), Amap (navigation), Meituan (food delivery), Ctrip (travel booking), Dianping (restaurant reviews)
  • Translation: Pleco (dictionary) and Baidu Translate are essential. Download offline packs

🍜 Dining Etiquette

  • Wait to be seated at restaurants
  • Use chopsticks properly — Never stick chopsticks upright in rice, don't point with them, don't tap bowls
  • Shared dishes — Meals are served family-style. Use serving chopsticks (公筷) when available
  • Leaving a little food shows the host has provided enough
  • Hot water — Chinese people drink hot/warm water. It's customary
  • Tipping — Not expected or practiced in China
  • Toasting — "Gānbēi" (干杯) means "dry cup" — bottoms up!

💰 Money & Budget

  • Currency: Chinese Yuan (CNY/RMB). Denominations: ¥1, ¥5, ¥10, ¥20, ¥50, ¥100
  • ATMs — Available everywhere. International cards work at major bank ATMs

🏥 Safety & Health

  • China is very safe for tourists. Violent crime against foreigners is extremely rare
  • Emergency numbers: Police 110, Ambulance 120, Fire 119
  • Travel insurance — Highly recommended
  • Air quality — Northern cities can be polluted; bring a mask. Southern/western China has excellent air quality
  • Pharmacies — Widely available. Many medicines available without prescription

📸 Photography Tips

  • Ask permission before photographing people, especially ethnic minorities and monks
  • Some attractions require separate photography tickets
  • Drone regulations — Heavily restricted. Registration required. No flying near government buildings or military areas
  • Best spots: Sunrise at the Great Wall, Li River at dusk, Zhangye Danxia at sunset
