Henan Province

Birthplace of Chinese civilization — Longmen Grottoes, Shaolin Temple, and the ancient capitals of Luoyang and Kaifeng.

Home / Provinces / Henan Province

Province Overview

Birthplace of Chinese civilization — Longmen Grottoes, Shaolin Temple, and the ancient capitals of Luoyang and Kaifeng.

📍 Location

Central China, the heart of the Central Plains (Zhongyuan). Crossroads of ancient Chinese civilization along the Yellow River.

👥 Population

Approximately 100 million — China's most populous province. The cradle of Chinese culture for 5,000 years.

🌡️ Climate

Temperate continental. Four distinct seasons. Hot summers, cold winters. Best visited Mar-May or Sep-Nov.

🏔️ Terrain

Flat plains in the east (North China Plain), mountains in the west (Songshan, Yuntai, Funiu). Yellow River runs through.

🍜 Cuisine

Henan noodles (huimian), soup dumplings, Luoyang water banquet (24-course meal), and Kaifeng street food.

✈️ Major Airport

Zhengzhou Xinzheng (CGO) — major hub connecting to domestic and some international destinations.

Why Visit Henan Province?

🗿 Longmen Grottoes

Over 100,000 Buddhist statues carved into limestone cliffs along the Yi River — China's finest sculptural art spanning 400 years. UNESCO listed.

🥋 Shaolin Temple

The legendary birthplace of kung fu — 1,500 years of martial arts tradition, pagoda forest, and daily monk demonstrations.

🏯 Kaifeng

Ancient Song Dynasty capital — the Qingming Shanghe Garden recreation, night markets, and Iron Pagoda.

🏔️ Yuntai Mountain

Jiaozuo's dramatic canyon scenery — waterfalls, red sandstone gorges, and emerald pools. A geological wonderland.

🏛️ Henan Museum

One of China's top museums — 130,000 artifacts including 9 national treasures spanning prehistoric to Qing Dynasty.

🌊 Yellow River

China's mother river — the dramatic Hukou Waterfall and the ancient flood control heritage along its banks.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Best season. Pleasant temperatures, peony flowers in Luoyang (April), and comfortable for temple visits.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid (35°C+). Best to avoid. Good for indoor museum visits and evening street food.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Second-best season. Cool and dry. Beautiful fall colors at Yuntai Mountain and Shaolin.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold and dry (-5 to 5°C). Few tourists. Lower prices but some outdoor areas less enjoyable.

Getting Around Henan Province

✈️ By Air

  • Airport: Zhengzhou is a major aviation hub with connections across China and some international routes.

🚄 By Train

  • Railway: Zhengzhou is China's most important rail junction. High-speed to Beijing (2.5h), Xi'an (2h), Wuhan (2h).

🚌 By Bus

  • Bus: Good bus network connecting all cities. Zhengzhou to Shaolin (1.5h), Kaifeng (1h), Luoyang (2h).

🚗 By Car

  • Driving: Excellent highway network. Self-driving between Zhengzhou, Luoyang, and Kaifeng is very convenient.

Henan Province Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Luoyang Peonies: Visit in mid-April for the Luoyang Peony Festival — millions of flowers in full bloom. The city's signature event.
  • Shaolin Tips: Arrive early to catch the morning martial arts demonstration. The pagoda forest is equally impressive.
  • Longmen Grottoes: Spend 3-4 hours. The Vairocana Buddha (Fengxian Temple) is the masterpiece. Go afternoon for best light.
  • Kaifeng Night Market: The Gulou Night Market is one of China's best. Try tangbao (soup dumplings) and guantangbao.
  • Henan Museum: Allow 3 hours minimum. The nine national treasures include bronze vessels from the Shang Dynasty.
  • Yuntai Mountain: The Red Stone Gorge (Hongshi Xia) is the highlight — allow 2-3 hours for the full scenic area.
  • Ancient Capitals: Henan was home to multiple ancient Chinese capitals. Zhengzhou, Luoyang, Kaifeng, and Anyang all have deep history.
  • Baijiu Culture: Henan has a strong drinking culture. Local baijiu is potent — pace yourself at banquets.
