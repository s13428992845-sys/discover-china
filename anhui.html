Anhui Province

Yellow Mountain's iconic granite peaks, ancient Huizhou villages, and the spiritual landscapes of Chinese ink paintings.

Home / Provinces / Anhui Province

Province Overview

Yellow Mountain's iconic granite peaks, ancient Huizhou villages, and the spiritual landscapes of Chinese ink paintings.

📍 Location

Eastern China, straddling the Yangtze and Huai rivers. Borders Jiangsu, Zhejiang, Jiangxi, Hubei, and Henan.

👥 Population

Approximately 61 million. Rich in history, home to the influential Huizhou merchant culture.

🌡️ Climate

Subtropical monsoon in the south, temperate in the north. Four seasons. Best visited Mar-May and Sep-Nov.

🏔️ Terrain

Southern Anhui features the famous Huangshan mountains. Northern Anhui is flat agricultural plains.

🍜 Cuisine

Anhui cuisine — one of China's Eight Great Cuisines. Known for braised dishes, stews, and mountain delicacies.

✈️ Major Airport

Hefei Xinqiao (HFE) — provincial hub. Huangshan Tunxi (TXN) for Yellow Mountain visitors.

Why Visit Anhui Province?

⛰️ Yellow Mountain

Huangshan — China's most iconic mountain. Granite peaks, twisted pines, sea of clouds, and hot springs. UNESCO World Heritage.

🏘️ Hongcun Village

UNESCO-listed ancient Hui village — white walls, black tiles, moon pond, and stunning mountain backdrop.

🏘️ Xidi Village

Another UNESCO ancient village — perfectly preserved Hui architecture with intricate wood carvings.

🏛️ Tunxi Old Street

A 'living Song Dynasty street' in Huangshan city — antique shops, ink stones, and traditional tea houses.

🏔️ Jiuhua Mountain

One of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains — 99 temples amidst stunning peaks.

🌊 Huizhou Architecture

Scattered throughout southern Anhui — ancestral halls, memorial arches, and villages that inspired ink painters for centuries.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Best season for Huangshan. Sea of clouds is most frequent. Rapeseed flowers bloom in March.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Green and lush on Huangshan. Hot in lowlands. Escape to mountain coolness.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Spectacular fall colors on Yellow Mountain. Clear skies and comfortable hiking weather.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Huangshan's snow-covered pines and peaks are magical. Fewer crowds. Cold but rewarding.

Getting Around Anhui Province

✈️ By Air

  • Airports: Hefei Xinqiao (HFE) for provincial access. Huangshan Tunxi (TXN) for mountain visitors. Flights from major cities.

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Hefei to Shanghai (3h), Nanjing (1.5h), Huangshan (2h). Excellent rail connectivity.
  • Huangshan North: HSR station with direct trains from Shanghai, Hangzhou, and Beijing.

🚌 By Bus

  • Local Buses: Regular buses from Huangshan city to Tangkou (Yellow Mountain base) and ancient villages.
  • Scenic Routes: Bus routes connect Hongcun, Xidi, and other villages.

🚗 By Car

  • Self-Driving: Good highways. The drive from Huangshan to the ancient villages is scenic and convenient.
  • Mountain Roads: Well-maintained roads to Yellow Mountain scenic area.

Anhui Province Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Yellow Mountain: Allow 2 days and stay overnight on the summit to catch sunrise and sea of clouds.
  • Hongcun at Dawn: Arrive before 7AM to photograph the moon pond reflection without crowds.
  • Pack Properly: Huangshan weather changes rapidly. Bring rain gear, warm layers, and good hiking shoes.
  • Huizhou Culture: Rent a guide in the ancient villages to understand the wood carvings and architecture.
  • Food Try: Stinky tofu (mao tofu), stone-ear mushroom stew, and Huangshan sesame cake are local specialties.
  • Best Photography: Sunrise from Bright Summit (Guangming Ding) or Start-to-Trust Peak (Shixin Feng).
  • Combine Trip: Huangshan + Hongcun/Xidi + Tunxi Old Street makes a perfect 3-day itinerary.
  • Cable Car: Two cable cars on Huangshan save 3+ hours of climbing. Use them for descent at least.
