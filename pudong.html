Pudong New Area

Shanghai's futuristic financial district — home to three of the world's tallest buildings and cutting-edge architecture.

Home / Shanghai / Pudong New Area

City Overview

Pudong transformed from farmland to one of the world's most impressive skylines in just 30 years. The Lujiazui financial district features the Shanghai Tower (632m), Shanghai World Financial Center, Jin Mao Tower, and the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower. It's a must-visit for architecture and city-view enthusiasts.

📍 Location

East bank of the Huangpu River. Shanghai's modern financial center.

🏙️ Skyline

Home to 3 of the world's top 10 tallest buildings. Unforgettable city views.

🌡️ Best Time

Clear days offer the best visibility from observation decks. Sunset for dramatic photos.

🏛️ Character

Ultra-modern business district with shopping malls, museums, and riverside parks.

Top Attractions

🗼 Shanghai Tower

China's tallest building (632m). The 118th-floor observation deck offers 360° city views up to 100km.

🔮 Oriental Pearl Tower

Shanghai's most iconic landmark with glass-floor observation deck and revolving restaurant.

🏛️ Shanghai Museum East

New branch of the famous museum in Pudong. World-class Chinese art and artifacts.

🌳 Lujiazui Park

Riverside green space with the best angles for photographing the Bund across the water.

Best Time to Visit Pudong New Area

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Clear skies often. Comfortable for walking the riverside promenade.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid. Best for evening visits and night photography.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best visibility for observation decks. Clear days with great photos.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold but crisp. Best visibility but can be hazy. Fewer crowds.

Pudong New Area Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Sunset Strategy: Visit Shanghai Tower at sunset — watch the city transition from day to spectacular night lights.
  • Combination Ticket: Buy combo tickets for multiple towers. The Shanghai Tower + History Museum offers good value.
  • Photo Tip: The best photos of Pudong are actually FROM the Bund, and vice versa. Visit both.
  • Metro Access: Lujiazui Station (Line 2) puts you right in the heart of the financial district.
