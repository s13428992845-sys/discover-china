The Bund (Waitan)

Shanghai's iconic waterfront promenade — a living museum of colonial-era architecture facing the futuristic Pudong skyline.

Home / Shanghai / The Bund (Waitan)

City Overview

The Bund is Shanghai's most famous landmark — a 1.5km waterfront promenade lined with 52 historic buildings in various European architectural styles. Built during the city's era as an international trading port, it offers a unique juxtaposition of 19th-century grandeur against the 21st-century Pudong skyline across the Huangpu River.

📍 Location

West bank of the Huangpu River, central Shanghai. The city's most iconic waterfront.

📏 Length

1.5km promenade with 52 heritage buildings. Easily walkable.

🏛️ Architecture

Neo-Classical, Art Deco, Baroque, Gothic — a 'Museum of World Architecture'.

🌃 Best Time

Sunset to evening — watch both sides light up for the full Shanghai experience.

Top Attractions

🏛️ HSBC Building

The grandest Bund building — now houses the Pudong Development Bank. Stunning interior dome.

🕐 Custom House

Famous clock tower modeled on Big Ben. Chimes the Westminster Quarters every quarter hour.

🌃 Night Cruise

Huangpu River cruises depart from Bund wharves. See both shores illuminated at night.

🛍️ Nanjing Road

China's premier shopping street connects to the Bund's western end. 5.5km of retail therapy.

Best Time to Visit The Bund (Waitan)

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Pleasant for walking. Occasional rain. Beautiful light for photography.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and crowded. Evening visits are much more comfortable.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable temperatures, great photography light.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold and windy on the waterfront. Dress warmly but crowds are thin.

The Bund (Waitan) Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Timing: Arrive 30 minutes before sunset to catch both daytime architecture and nighttime illumination.
  • Cross the River: Take the public ferry (2 CNY) across to Pudong — cheapest cruise with great views.
  • Photos: The best Bund photos face west toward the colonial buildings, or east toward Pudong at night.
  • Avoid Weekends: Weekday mornings are least crowded. Weekend evenings can be packed.
