Beijing

The heart of China — 3,000 years of history, the Forbidden City, Great Wall, and a thriving modern metropolis where ancient traditions meet cutting-edge culture.

Home / Provinces / Beijing

Province Overview

Beijing, China's capital for over 800 years, is a city of extraordinary contrasts. Ancient imperial palaces and temples stand alongside ultramodern architecture and vibrant neighborhoods. Home to 7 UNESCO World Heritage Sites, it remains one of the world's most historically significant cities.

📍 Location

Northern China, surrounded by Hebei Province. Political and cultural center of China for centuries.

👥 Population

Approximately 22 million. One of the world's most populous capital cities.

🌡️ Climate

Continental monsoon. Hot humid summers, cold dry winters. Best: Apr-May and Sep-Oct for clear skies.

🏔️ Terrain

Mountains to the northwest, plains to the southeast. Average elevation 43m. Great Wall runs through northern mountains.

🍜 Cuisine

Peking Duck, jianbing, zhajiangmian, dumplings, and imperial cuisine. One of China's greatest food cities.

✈️ Major Airport

Beijing Capital (PEK) and Daxing (PKX) — two world-class international airports.

Why Visit Beijing?

🏯 Forbidden City

The world's largest palace complex — 980 buildings, 9,000+ rooms. Home to 24 emperors over 500 years.

🧱 Great Wall

Multiple sections accessible: Badaling (popular), Mutianyu (scenic), Jinshanling (hiking), Simatai (wild).

🏛️ Temple of Heaven

Masterpiece of Ming Dynasty architecture. Emperors prayed here for good harvests. Beautiful surrounding park.

🏘️ Hutongs

Ancient alleyway neighborhoods offering authentic Beijing life. Explore by rickshaw or on foot.

🏮 Summer Palace

Imperial garden with Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill. A masterpiece of Chinese landscape design.

🏟️ Olympic Legacy

Bird's Nest, Water Cube, and Olympic Park from 2008. Also host of 2022 Winter Olympics.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (March - May)

Warming up, flowers blooming. Occasional sandstorms in March. Ideal for Great Wall hiking.

☀️ Summer (June - August)

Hot and humid, 30-35°C. Rainy season. Crowded but vibrant. Great for parks and evening activities.

🍂 Autumn (September - November)

Golden autumn — the BEST season. Clear blue skies, comfortable temperatures, stunning foliage.

❄️ Winter (December - February)

Cold and dry, -5 to 5°C. Fewer tourists. Great for hot pot and indoor cultural experiences.

Getting Around Beijing

✈️ By Air

  • International: Capital (PEK) and Daxing (PKX) airports serve global destinations
  • Airport Express: 20-min train from Capital Airport to city center
  • Daxing Access: New Daxing Airport connected by high-speed train (19 min to Caoqiao)

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Connected to all major cities: Shanghai 4.5h, Xi'an 4.5h, Guangzhou 8h
  • Beijing Station: Historic station for trains to northeast China and Russia
  • Beijing West: Main station for southern and western routes

🚇 By Subway

  • Network: 27 lines, 800+ km — one of the world's largest metro systems
  • Coverage: Reaches all major attractions. Most efficient way to get around
  • Cost: 2-10 CNY per trip. Use Yitongxing app or Beijing card

🚌 By Bus

  • Tourist Buses: Special lines to Great Wall, Ming Tombs, and other attractions
  • City Buses: Extensive network but can be crowded during rush hours
  • Night Buses: Available on major routes after subway closes

Beijing Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Book Ahead: Forbidden City and many attractions require advance online booking. Tickets sell out days ahead.
  • Best Wall Section: Mutianyu offers the best balance of scenery and crowds. Jinshanling for serious hikers.
  • Subway is King: Avoid surface traffic — the subway is fast, clean, and reaches almost everywhere.
  • Hutong Experience: Visit Nanluoguxiang or Shichahai area for authentic hutong life. Try a rickshaw tour.
  • Street Food: Try jianbing (breakfast crepe), baozi (steamed buns), and lamb skewers in Wangfujing area.
  • Pollution Check: Download an air quality app. On bad days, plan indoor activities.
  • Language: English is limited outside tourist areas. Have your hotel card and destinations written in Chinese.
  • Temple Fair: Visit during Spring Festival for traditional temple fairs with performances and snacks.
