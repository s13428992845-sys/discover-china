Guizhou Province

Known as the "Land of Serenity" — discover dramatic karst landscapes, Asia's largest waterfall, and 17 vibrant ethnic minority cultures in China's most affordable hidden gem.

Home / Provinces / Guizhou

Province Overview

Guizhou is a landlocked province in Southwest China known for dramatic karst landscapes, the massive Huangguoshu Waterfall, and rich ethnic minority cultures. It's one of China's most affordable and undiscovered destinations, offering authentic experiences away from mass tourism.

📍 Location

Southwest China, bordered by Yunnan, Sichuan, Chongqing, Hunan, and Guangxi. Gateway to Southwest.

👥 Population

Approximately 38 million, with 17 ethnic minority groups including Miao, Dong, and Buyi.

🌡️ Climate

Mild and humid, average 15°C. Best visited May-October with cool summers.

🏔️ Terrain

Karst mountains, deep valleys, caves, and waterfalls. Part of the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau.

🍜 Cuisine

Sour and spicy flavors — sour fish soup (酸汤鱼), spicy dipping sauces, and glutinous rice.

✈️ Major Airport

Guiyang Longdongbao International Airport (KWE) — gateway to the province.

Why Visit Guizhou?

🌊 Huangguoshu Waterfall

Asia's largest waterfall, 77.8m high and 101m wide. Walk behind the water curtain for an unforgettable experience!

🏘️ Ethnic Minority Villages

Xijiang Miao Village (world's largest), Zhaoxing Dong Village with famous drum towers, and more authentic experiences.

🏔️ Karst Landscapes

Limestone formations, underground caves, and natural bridges. Zhijin Cave is absolutely spectacular.

🌿 Cool Summer Escape

One of China's "cool capitals" with summer temperatures rarely exceeding 30°C. Perfect summer retreat!

🍲 Sour & Spicy Cuisine

Unique Guizhou flavors: sour fish soup, fermented fish, and incredible chili-based dishes you won't find elsewhere.

📸 Photography Paradise

Misty mountains, terraced fields, colorful minority festivals. A photographer's dream destination!

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (March - May)

Pleasant weather, flowers blooming, minority festivals begin. Great for outdoor activities and photography.

☀️ Summer (June - August)

Cool escape from heat, waterfalls at peak flow, but rainy season. Perfect for beating the heat!

🍂 Autumn (September - November)

Harvest season, clear skies, rice terraces turn golden. Peak travel season with stunning scenery.

❄️ Winter (December - February)

Quiet season, fewer tourists, mild but damp. Good for budget travelers seeking authentic experiences.

Getting Around Guizhou

✈️ By Air

  • Major Airport: Guiyang Longdongbao (KWE) — domestic and regional flights
  • Regional Airports: Zunyi, Anshun, and Libo airports for specific routes
  • Budget Flights: Often cheaper than train for long distances

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Connects to Chengdu, Chongqing, Changsha, and Kunming
  • Scenic Routes: Guiyang to surrounding attractions offer beautiful views
  • Local Trains: Affordable option for getting around the province

🚌 By Bus

  • Long-Distance Buses: Extensive network to smaller towns and villages
  • Sleeper Buses: Available for overnight journeys between cities
  • Local Mini-Buses: To remote villages and scenic areas

🚗 Car Rental / 包车

  • Self-Drive: Mountain roads can be challenging but scenic
  • Hire with Driver: Popular option, especially for minority villages
  • Tour Groups: Organized day trips from Guiyang available

Guizhou Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Rain Gear Essential: "No three days without rain" is a local saying. Always carry an umbrella or rain jacket.
  • Mountain Roads: Winding roads are common — motion sickness medication is recommended for long trips.
  • Respect Minority Customs: Ask before photographing locals, and remove shoes in some homes and temples.
  • Village Entrance Fees: Some villages charge 80-100 CNY — worth it for the authentic cultural experience.
  • Rice Wine Warning: Try the local rice wine, but be careful — it's stronger than it tastes!
  • Offline Maps: Download offline maps before heading to mountain areas — signal can be weak.
  • Comfortable Shoes: Bring sturdy walking shoes — lots of stairs in villages and scenic areas.
  • Hire Local Guides: Highly recommended for minority villages — greatly enhances the experience and understanding.
