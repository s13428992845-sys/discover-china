Dali

A romantic blend of ancient Bai culture, stunning mountain-lake scenery, and bohemian cafes. Discover the "Wind, Flower, Snow, and Moon" of Yunnan.

Home / Yunnan / Dali

City Overview

📍 Location

Located in western Yunnan, 300km from Kunming. Nestled between Cangshan Mountains and Erhai Lake. Ancient capital of the Nanzhao Kingdom.

👥 Population

~700,000 in Dali City. The Old Town area has ~50,000 permanent residents, swelling with tourists seasonally.

🏛️ Character

Bai ethnic minority culture, well-preserved ancient architecture, cobblestone streets, traditional "Three Pagodas" temples. Popular with artists and digital nomads.

🌡️ Climate

Mild year-round. Spring-like in all seasons. Best: March-June for flowers, September-November for weather. "The wind is strong year-round" — bring a jacket.

💡 Why Visit Dali?

  • Scenery: Perfect framing of mountains behind the old town, Erhai Lake reflections
  • Culture: Living Bai culture —Architecture, tie-dye, festivals, traditional customs
  • Bohemian Vibes: Cafes, art galleries, creative shops, backpacker community
  • Outdoor Activities: Cycling Erhai Lake, hiking Cangshan, hot springs
  • Photography: Golden sunrise at Three Pagodas, sunset over Erhai, flower fields

Top Attractions

Dali Ancient Town

Dali Ancient Town

UNESCO World Heritage site featuring 1,200 years of history. Cobblestone streets, Bai-style architecture with wooden houses and fireplaces, traditional "_foreign trade" shops. Famous for Wohua Mountain (五华楼) — climb for panoramic views. Best visited early morning or evening to avoid crowds.

💰 Ticket: Free (some attractions inside)
🕐 Best Time: Early morning or evening
📍 Location: City Center
UNESCO History Nightlife
Three Pagodas of Dali

Three Pagodas (San Ta)

Iconic Buddhist temples built during the Tang Dynasty (9th century). The three pagodas stand in a triangular formation — the main pagoda is 69 meters tall. Surrounded by a peaceful temple complex with ancient cypress trees. The backdrop is often photographed with Cangshan Mountains.

💰 Ticket: ¥75 (combined ticket with other sites)
🕐 Open: 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM
📍 Distance: 1 km north of old town
Buddhism Photography History
Erhai Lake Cycling

Erhai Lake Cycling

Yunnan's second-largest highland lake (268 sq km). A 120km scenic road circles the lake with breathtaking views of Cangshan Mountains. Best done by electric bike (¥60-80/day) or regular bicycle. Stop at Fisherman's Wharf, Shuangqiao Old Town, and small fishing villages along the way.

💰 Cost: ¥60-100 (bike rental)
🕐 Duration: Half-day to full day
📍 Route: Around the lake
Nature Cycling Scenery
Xizhou Old Town

Xizhou Old Town

Well-preserved Bai village 30km north of Dali. Famous for its traditional courtyard homes, the largest of which is the Zhou's Family Mansion (周家大院). Try "Bai three-course meal" (白族三道菜) and learn about Bai tea ceremonies. Much less touristy than Dali Old Town.

💰 Ticket: Free (mansion ¥20)
🕐 Open: All day
📍 Distance: 30 km north
Culture Authentic Food
Cangshan Mountain

Cangshan Mountain (Zhonghe Temple)

Part of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau, rising to 4,122 meters. Take the感通索道 (Guantong Cable Car) for stunning views, or hike the 15km ridge trail for the full experience. Ancient temples hidden in the forest. Weather changes quickly — bring layers and rain gear.

💰 Ticket: ¥35 (park) + ¥80 (cable car)
🕐 Open: 8:30 AM - 5:00 PM
⚠️ Altitude: 2,000-4,000m
Hiking Nature Adventure

Local Food & Restaurants

Bai cuisine with fresh ingredients, unique flavors, and beautiful presentation

🥘

Bai Three-Course Meal (Bai San Tao)

Traditional Bai cuisine: cold mixed vegetables, steamed meat with Yunnan ham, and soup with local greens. Served with rice and pickles. Best experienced at families' homes in Xizhou or try at "Yunshan Bai Restaurant."

🧀

Erhai Fish (Erhai Yu)

Fresh fish from Erhai Lake, typically served steamed with pickled vegetables or in hot pot. The fish is tender and sweet. Head to restaurants along the lake or at Fisherman's Wharf for the freshest catch.

🥣

Bai Tie Pi (Iron Billet Noodles)

Legend says the noodles are so tough they can only be cut with a knife. Hand-pulled noodles served in rich broth with cilantro, chili, and pickled vegetables. A Dali breakfast staple.

🍵

Three-Tea-One-Flower Ceremony

Bai tea ceremony — three types of tea: bitter (wind), sweet (snow), and honey-flower tea. Usually served with Bai snacks at traditional homes. Ask your guesthouse host to arrange a ceremony.

🥟

Bai Cold Set (Bai Liang Pan)

Cold dish arrangement with seasonal vegetables, tofu, mushrooms, and specialty items arranged artfully. Served with tangy sauce. Refreshing in summer, available year-round.

🍽️ Where to Eat

  • Foreigner's Street (Yangbi Road): Range of international and fusion restaurants
  • 复兴大道 (Fuxing Avenue): Upscale Yunnan and Bai cuisine
  • Yeyik Cannery: Hipster cafes and creative spaces
  • Erhai Lake Restaurants: Fresh fish, casual atmosphere, lake views

Transportation Guide

🚄 Getting to Dali

  • Train from Kunming: Dali Station (¥145, 2.5 hrs high-speed). Then bus #8 or Didi to old town (¥30).
  • Bus from Kunming: Xiaguan Bus Station (¥130-150, 4-5 hrs). Closer to old town.
  • Flight: Dali Airport (DLU) — limited routes, mostly via Kunming.

🛵 Getting Around

  • Electric Bike: Best way to explore! ¥60-100/day. Rent at shops near North Gate. License not required under 20km/h.
  • Didi/Taxi: Available but less convenient in old town. ¥10-30 within area.
  • Bus: Bus #4 connects old town to Three Pagodas (¥2). C2 bus to Xizhou.
  • Foot: Old town is walkable. Best for exploring the streets and lanes.

🚗 Day Trips

  • Shuangqiao Old Town: 15km east, charming fishing village with budget cafes
  • Xizhou: 30km north, authentic Bai culture and architecture
  • Weishan: 60km north, less-visited ancient town with 100+ temples
  • Shenhai: Ancient salt well, remote and authentic experience

💡 Tips

  • Rent bikes from guesthouses — often cheaper and includes delivery
  • Strong wind in afternoon — choose enclosed electric bikes if sensitive
  • For Erhai Lake cycling, start early to avoid wind and crowds
  • Taxi drivers may not speak English — have addresses ready in Chinese

  • 11:30 AM Cangshan Mountain cable car — panoramic views of lake
  • 2:00 PM Cycle Erhai Lake — stop at Shuangqiao for lunch
  • 5:00 PM Return to town, explore shops and cafes
  • 7:30 PM Dinner — Bai cuisine, people-watching on Foreigner's Street
