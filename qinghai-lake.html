Qinghai Lake Area

China's largest lake — vast blue waters, grasslands, rapeseed flowers, and Tibetan nomadic culture.

Home / Qinghai Province / Qinghai Lake Area

City Overview

Qinghai Lake is China's largest inland lake — a vast turquoise expanse at 3,200m surrounded by grasslands where Tibetan nomads graze yaks. In July, millions of acres of golden rapeseed flowers create one of China's most spectacular landscapes.

📍 Location

150km west of Xining. At 3,200m elevation, 100km long and 60km wide.

🌊 Scale

China's largest lake — 4,543 km². So vast it looks like an ocean. Sacred to both Tibetans and Mongols.

🌻 Flowers

In July, the northeast shore is carpeted with golden rapeseed flowers against blue lake and sky.

🏕️ Nomads

Tibetan and Mongol nomads still live in yak-hair tents around the lake. Visit for authentic cultural exchange.

Top Attractions

🏖️ Bird Island

Home to 100,000+ migratory birds (May-Jul). Bar-headed geese, brown-headed gulls, and cormorants.

🌻 Flower Sea

The northeast shore in July — golden rapeseed fields stretching to the blue lake. Iconic Qinghai image.

🏕️ Nomad Visit

Stay with Tibetan nomads in yak-hair tents. Drink yak butter tea and experience plateau life.

🚴 Lake Cycling

The annual Qinghai Lake International Cycling Race. You can cycle sections of the 360km lakeshore road.

Best Time to Visit Qinghai Lake Area

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Lake is at its lowest. Cold but clear. Migratory birds start arriving in May.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Best season. Warm days (15-20°C), rapeseed flowers in July. Cycling race in July-August.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Golden grasslands. Fewer crowds. Clear blue lake. Temperature drops rapidly in October.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Extremely cold. Lake partially freezes. Few visitors but dramatic frozen landscapes.

Qinghai Lake Area Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Circumnavigate: The full loop is 360km. You can drive it in a day or cycle over 2-3 days.
  • Flower Season: Mid-July is peak for rapeseed flowers. The area between Gangcha and Erlangjian is best.
  • Stay Warm: Even in summer, lakeside nights drop to 5°C. Bring warm layers.
  • Nomad Etiquette: Ask permission before photographing nomads. Bring small gifts (tea, candy) for families.
