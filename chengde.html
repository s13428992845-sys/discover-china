Chengde

Imperial summer retreat — the Qing dynasty's magnificent Mountain Resort and Outer Eight Temples.

Home / Hebei Province / Chengde

City Overview

Chengde was the Qing Dynasty emperors' summer retreat — escaping Beijing's heat in this mountain paradise. The Mountain Resort is China's largest imperial garden, and the surrounding 'Outer Eight Temples' combine Chinese, Tibetan, and Mongol architectural styles.

📍 Location

Northeastern Hebei, 230km from Beijing. Nestled in mountains at 350m elevation. Cooler than Beijing.

🏛️ Mountain Resort

China's largest imperial garden — 560 hectares of palaces, lakes, mountains, and gardens. UNESCO listed.

🏛️ Outer Temples

Eight spectacular temples combining Han, Tibetan, and Mongol styles — including a replica of the Potala Palace.

🌡️ Climate

2-3°C cooler than Beijing in summer — the reason emperors retreated here. Four distinct seasons.

Top Attractions

🏛️ Mountain Resort

Explore palaces, lake districts, mountain trails, and plain areas — this was the Qing's summer capital.

🏛️ Puning Temple

Modeled after Tibet's Samye Monastery — houses the world's tallest wooden Guanyin statue (27m).

🏛️ Xumi Fushou

A replica of the Tashilhunpo Monastery in Tibet — built for the Panchen Lama's visit to the emperor.

🏔️ Hot Springs

Natural hot springs in the area — the emperors enjoyed them, and you can too at local resorts.

Best Time to Visit Chengde

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Flowers blooming in the resort gardens. Few tourists — peaceful exploration.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Peak season. The resort is 2-3°C cooler than Beijing. Book accommodation early.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Stunning fall colors in the mountains. Comfortable temperatures.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Very cold (-15 to -5°C). Resort closed in winter. Hot springs are the main draw.

Chengde Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Allow Full Day: The Mountain Resort is enormous — you need at least 6 hours to see the highlights.
  • Temple Circuit: Visit at least Puning Temple and Xumi Fushou — both are masterpieces of multicultural architecture.
  • From Beijing: Take the 2-hour high-speed train from Beijing. Perfect for a 2-3 day trip.
  • Hot Springs: After a day exploring the resort, soak in the natural hot springs — a imperial tradition.
