Zhangjiajie

Avatar's real-world inspiration — towering sandstone pillars, glass bridge, Tianmen Mountain, and ethnic minority culture.

Home / Hunan Province / Zhangjiajie

City Overview

Zhangjiajie is one of China's most spectacular natural wonders — thousands of towering sandstone pillars covered in lush vegetation inspired the floating mountains in the movie Avatar. The area also features the world's longest and highest glass bridge.

📍 Location

Northwestern Hunan, bordering Hubei. 4 hours from Changsha by HSR.

🏔️ Avatar Mountains

3,000+ sandstone pillars — the 'Southern Sky Column' inspired the floating mountains in Avatar.

🌉 Glass Bridge

The world's longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge — 430m long, 300m above the gorge.

🎭 Tujia Culture

Home to Tujia, Bai, and Miao ethnic minorities — colorful festivals and unique customs.

Top Attractions

🏔️ Avatar Hallelujah Mountain

The 'Southern Sky Column' renamed after Avatar — a 1,080m sandstone pillar. The park's iconic view.

🏔️ Tianmen Mountain

Heaven's Gate — a natural arch at the summit. The 7,455m cable car is the world's longest.

🌉 Glass Bridge

Walk on glass 300m above the gorge. Also features the world's highest bungee jump.

🎭 Tujia Performances

Traditional Tujia folk songs and dances — the 'crying marriage' custom is unique.

Best Time to Visit Zhangjiajie

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Misty and atmospheric. Waterfalls are at their fullest. Cloud sea views are common.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warm and green. Peak season — crowded but accessible. Cool on the mountain tops.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable temperatures. Spectacular visibility of the pillars.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold but dramatic. Snow-covered pillars are magical. Cable cars may close in ice.

Zhangjiajie Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Allow 3 Days: Day 1: Avatar Mountains (Zhangjiajie National Forest). Day 2: Tianmen Mountain. Day 3: Glass Bridge.
  • Early Start: Arrive at the park gates at 7AM to avoid crowds and catch the morning mist in the valleys.
  • Glass Bridge: Book tickets online in advance — limited daily visitors. Wear shoe covers (provided).
  • Tianmen Tips: Take the world's longest cable car. The 999 steps to Heaven's Gate are steep — take it slow.
