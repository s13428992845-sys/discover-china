Mount Hua (Huashan)

One of China's Five Great Sacred Mountains — famous for the world's most dangerous hiking trail with cliffside plank walks and Daoist temples perched on sheer rock faces.

Home / Shaanxi / Huashan

📍 Top Attractions

Chang Kong Plank Walk (长空栈道)

The famous cliffside plank walk — narrow wooden boards bolted into a vertical cliff face at 2,160m. Not for the faint-hearted!

Five Peaks (五峰)

Explore all five summits: East (best sunrise), South (highest at 2,154m), West, North, and Central peaks — each with unique views.

Jade Spring Temple (玉泉院)

The starting point of the traditional hiking trail, this Daoist temple has welcomed pilgrims for over 1,000 years.

🎫 Tickets

  • Peak Season (Mar-Nov): ¥160
  • Off Season (Dec-Feb): ¥100
  • North Peak Cable Car: ¥80 one way
  • West Peak Cable Car: ¥140 one way
  • Plank Walk: ¥30 (includes safety harness rental)

🚄 Getting There

🚄 By High-Speed Rail

  • Xi'an North to Huashan North Station: ~30 min
  • Free shuttle bus to visitor center

🚌 By Bus

  • Xi'an East Bus Station to Huashan
  • ~2 hours

💡 Travel Tips

  • Night hike up North Peak to arrive for sunrise at East Peak — the most popular strategy
  • Bring gloves and headlamp — many sections use chains for support
  • April–October is best. Avoid in rain/snow for safety
  • Pack light — every gram counts on steep stair sections
