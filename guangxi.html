Guangxi

Karst landscapes of unparalleled beauty, Zhuang minority culture, and the legendary Li River.

Home / Provinces / Guangxi

Province Overview

Karst landscapes of unparalleled beauty, Zhuang minority culture, and the legendary Li River.

📍 Location

Southern China, bordering Vietnam. A region of dramatic karst mountains and ethnic diversity.

⛰️ Karst Wonderland

Home to some of Earth's most spectacular karst landscapes — limestone peaks, caves, and rivers.

🎵 Zhuang Culture

The Zhuang are China's largest ethnic minority — famous for their singing, brocade, and festivals.

🌊 Li River

The Li River cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo is one of China's most iconic journeys.

Why Visit Guangxi?

🏞️ Li River Cruise

The 83km cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo — dramatic karst peaks rising from emerald waters.

🌙 Moon Hill

A natural arch at the top of a hill in Yangshuo — climb for sunset views over the karst landscape.

🏔️ Longji Rice Terraces

Stunning terraced rice paddies carved into mountainsides — 'Dragon's Backbone' — 700 years old.

🌊 Detian Waterfall

Asia's largest transnational waterfall — straddling the China-Vietnam border.

🏛️ Guangxi Museum

Home to the famous Bronze Drum culture — artifacts from 2,000 years of Zhuang civilization.

🌾 Yangshuo Countryside

Cycling through rice paddies and karst peaks — one of China's most beautiful rural landscapes.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Pleasant and misty. Karst peaks shrouded in clouds create a magical atmosphere. Rice terraces begin flooding.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and rainy. Rivers are high — ideal for Li River cruises. Lush green landscapes.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, golden rice terraces. Perfect for photography and cycling.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool and damp. Few tourists. Some snow possible on higher peaks. Quiet atmosphere.

Getting Around Guangxi

✈️ By Air

  • Guilin Liangjiang Airport (KWL) Main tourist gateway — flights from major Chinese cities.
  • Nanning Wuxu Airport (NNG) Regional hub with connections across China and to Southeast Asia.

🚄 By Train

  • Guilin Station HSR connects to Guangzhou (2.5h), Guiyang (2h), and other major cities.
  • Nanning Station HSR hub — 4.5h to Guangzhou, 13h to Beijing.

🚌 By Bus

  • Tourist Buses Regular services between Guilin, Yangshuo, and Longji Rice Terraces.
  • Local Transport Minibuses connect smaller towns and scenic areas.

🚢 By Boat

  • Li River Cruise The classic 4-5 hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo — the highlight of any Guangxi trip.
  • Yulong River Bamboo Raft Peaceful bamboo raft rides through the countryside near Yangshuo.

Guangxi Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Li River Cruise: Book the 4-star boat for comfort. The downstream route (Guilin→Yangshuo) offers the best scenery.
  • Yangshuo: Rent a bicycle or electric scooter to explore the countryside — far more rewarding than organized tours.
  • Longji Terraces: Best visited in June (water-filled) or September-October (golden harvest). Allow 2 days.
  • Festivals: The Zhuang 'San Yue San' festival (3rd day of 3rd lunar month) features traditional singing competitions.
  • Food: Try Guilin rice noodles (桂林米粉) — the local breakfast staple. Also try beer fish (啤酒鱼) in Yangshuo.
  • Caving: Guangxi has some of Asia's largest caves — Silver Cave near Yangshuo is spectacular.
  • Currency: In rural areas, carry cash — small vendors may not accept mobile payment.
  • Photography: Early morning mist over the Li River and karst peaks is the iconic shot — wake up early!
