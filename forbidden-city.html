Forbidden City

The world's largest palace complex — 980 buildings and 9,000+ rooms of imperial splendor spanning 500 years of Chinese history.

Home / Beijing / Forbidden City

City Overview

The Forbidden City, also known as the Palace Museum, served as the imperial palace for 24 emperors during the Ming and Qing dynasties. Built between 1406-1420, it is the world's largest and best-perved ancient wooden palace complex. Every hall, courtyard, and artifact tells a story of China's imperial past.

📍 Location

Central Beijing, directly north of Tiananmen Square.

⏰ Built

1406-1420 during the Ming Dynasty. Over 600 years of history.

📐 Size

72 hectares, 980 buildings, 9,000+ rooms. Surrounded by 10m walls and a moat.

🏛️ Significance

UNESCO World Heritage Site. China's most visited cultural attraction.

Top Attractions

🏛️ Hall of Supreme Harmony

The largest and most important hall — used for imperial coronations and major ceremonies.

🎨 Palace Museum

Over 1.8 million artifacts including paintings, ceramics, and jade. Rotating exhibitions.

🏘️ Imperial Gardens

The御花园 (Yuhuayuan) offers peaceful gardens with ancient trees and rockeries.

📸 Corner Tower

The iconic corner watchtower reflected in the moat — Beijing's most photographed spot.

Best Time to Visit Forbidden City

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Beautiful with blooming flowers in the gardens. Moderate crowds.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot but atmospheric. Early morning visits recommended.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season — golden ginkgo trees contrast with red walls. Peak beauty.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Snow on the palace roofs is magical. Fewer crowds, but very cold.

Forbidden City Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Book Tickets Online: Tickets MUST be booked online in advance — no on-site ticket sales. Book 7+ days ahead.
  • Enter from South: The main entrance is through the Meridian Gate (south). Exit from the Gate of Divine Prowess (north).
  • Allow 3-4 Hours: The complex is enormous. Wear comfortable shoes and bring water.
  • After Jingshan: Climb Jingshan Park hill across the north gate for the iconic panoramic view.
