Chengdu

The laid-back capital of Sichuan — home of giant pandas, world-famous spicy cuisine, ancient temples, and a 3,000-year-old teahouse culture.

Home / Sichuan / Chengdu

📍 Top Attractions

Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding

Get up close with China's national treasure. Best visited early morning when pandas are most active.

Jinli Ancient Street & Kuanzhai Alley

Wander through Qing Dynasty architecture, street food stalls, and traditional Sichuan opera teahouses.

Wuhou Shrine & Dufu's Thatched Cottage

Immerse yourself in Three Kingdoms history and the home of China's greatest poet.

🍜 Food & Cuisine

Chengdu is a UNESCO City of Gastronomy. Must-try dishes include Mapo Tofu, Kung Pao Chicken, Dan Dan Noodles, and the legendary Sichuan Hotpot. The city's street food scene on Jinli Street and around Sichuan University is legendary.

🚄 Getting There & Around

✈️ By Air

  • Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU)
  • Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU)
  • Direct flights from major global cities

🚄 By High-Speed Rail

  • Beijing to Chengdu: ~8 hours
  • Xi'an to Chengdu: ~3.5 hours
  • Chongqing to Chengdu: ~1.5 hours

🚇 Local Transport

  • 13 metro lines covering the city
  • Extensive bus network
  • Didi ride-hailing widely available

💡 Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: March–May or September–November
  • Visit the Panda Base before 9 AM for the best experience
  • Try the local tea culture at a traditional teahouse in People's Park
  • Bring an umbrella — Chengdu is known for its misty weather
