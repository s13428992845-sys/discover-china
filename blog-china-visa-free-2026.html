🛂 Skip the embassy — visit China visa-free with these transit and entry programs

✅ Quick Answer

54 countries can enter China visa-free for tourism in 2026. Additionally, citizens from 54 countries can use the 72/144-hour transit visa waiver.

✈️ Visa-Free Tourism (54 Countries)

China introduced visa-free tourism for citizens from 54 countries in 2024-2025, making it easier than ever to visit. This policy allows stays of up to 15 days for tourism purposes.

How It Works

Simply book your flight, pack your bags, and go. No embassy visit, no paperwork, no fees. When you arrive in China, show your passport and onward ticket, and you'll receive a visa-free entry stamp.

Eligible Countries (Visa-Free Tourism)

  • Europe: France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Switzerland, Austria, Belgium, Netherlands, Hungary, Ireland, Poland, Denmark, Sweden, Finland, Norway, Portugal, Greece
  • Asia: Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, South Korea, Japan, Brunei, Philippines
  • Oceania: Australia, New Zealand
  • Americas: USA, Canada, Mexico, Argentina, Brazil, Chile, Peru
  • Middle East: UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman
  • Africa: South Africa, Morocco, Egypt, Kenya

📌 Important Note

This list is updated as of June 2026. Always check with the Chinese embassy or official sources before travel, as policies may change.

🚄 144-Hour Transit Visa Waiver

The 144-hour (6-day) transit visa waiver allows you to explore China while in transit to a third country. This is perfect for layovers or multi-country trips.

Entry Points

  • Beijing: Beijing Capital (PEK), Daxing (PKX)
  • Shanghai: Pudong (PVG), Hongqiao (SHA)
  • Guangzhou: Baiyun (CAN)
  • Chengdu: Tianfu (TFU)
  • Shenzhen: Bao'an (SZX)
  • Kunming: Changshui (KMG)
  • Xiamen: Gaoqi (XMN)
  • Qingdao: Jiaodong (TAO)

🕐 72-Hour Transit Visa Waiver

The older 72-hour (3-day) program is still available in some cities with more flexible routing options.

  • Cities: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Shenyang, Dalian, Guilin, Kunming, Chongqing, Xi'an
  • Duration: 72 hours from entry
  • Routing: More flexible than 144-hour option

📋 Requirements & Restrictions

For Visa-Free Tourism

  • Valid passport (6+ months remaining)
  • Round-trip or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)
  • Maximum stay: 15 days
  • Cannot extend beyond 15 days
  • Must depart via international port

For Transit Visa Waiver

  • Valid passport (6+ months remaining)
  • Confirmed onward ticket to third country
  • Visa for third country (if required)
  • Stay within transit region
  • Depart within allowed time (72 or 144 hours)
  • Cannot work or study

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong routing: Beijing → Shanghai → London doesn't qualify (both in China)
  • Same-origin return: London → Beijing → London doesn't qualify
  • Overstaying: Even 1 hour over can result in fines or blacklisting
  • No hotel booking: Officers may ask for proof of accommodation

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I work in China with visa-free entry?

A: No. Visa-free entry is for tourism and transit only. Working requires a proper work visa.

Q: Can I extend my 15-day visa-free stay?

A: No, extensions are not permitted. You must depart within 15 days.

Q: Does visa-free entry apply to all Chinese ports?

A: Most international airports accept visa-free entry. Some smaller border crossings may have restrictions.

Q: Can I use visa-free entry multiple times per year?

A: Yes, there's no limit on the number of visa-free entries per year.

Q: What if my country isn't on the list?

A: You'll need to apply for a tourist visa at a Chinese embassy or use the transit visa waiver if transiting.

Q: Do I need travel insurance?

A: Not required for visa-free entry, but highly recommended for any international travel.
