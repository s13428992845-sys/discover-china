Zhengzhou

The central hub — Shaolin Temple nearby, excellent museum, and gateway to Henan's ancient wonders.

Home / Henan Province / Zhengzhou

City Overview

Zhengzhou is China's most important rail junction and the capital of Henan province. While often passed through rather than visited, it's home to an excellent museum, gateway to Shaolin Temple, and surrounded by the cradle of Chinese civilization.

📍 Location

Central Henan, on the Yellow River plain. China's most important rail crossroads.

🏛️ Museum

Henan Museum — one of China's top museums with 9 national treasures spanning 5,000 years of civilization.

🥋 Shaolin Gateway

Only 80km from the legendary Shaolin Temple and Mount Song — the martial arts capital.

🚄 Rail Hub

China's high-speed rail center — reach Beijing (2.5h), Xi'an (2h), Wuhan (2h) all within 2-3 hours.

Top Attractions

🏛️ Henan Museum

130,000 artifacts including the famous 'Flying Cloud' bronze horse and jade burial suits.

🥋 Shaolin Day Trip

The legendary kung fu temple is only 80km away — a perfect day trip from Zhengzhou.

🍜 Noodle Streets

Try Henan's signature huimian (braised noodles) — hearty, savory, and uniquely local.

🏛️ Shang Dynasty Ruins

The 3,600-year-old Shang Dynasty city walls in the city center — among the oldest in China.

Best Time to Visit Zhengzhou

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Best season. Comfortable 15-25°C. Perfect for museum visits and Shaolin day trips.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot (35-40°C). Indoor museum is best. Evening street food when it cools down.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Second-best season. Cool and clear. Great for combining multiple Henan cities.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold (-5 to 5°C). Good for museum visits and warm noodle soup.

Zhengzhou Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Henan Museum: Allow 3-4 hours. Don't miss the 9 national treasures — they tell the story of Chinese civilization.
  • Shaolin Day Trip: Leave by 7AM, catch the morning martial arts demo. Return by evening. Regular buses available.
  • Shang Ruins: The 3,600-year-old city walls are in the center of modern Zhengzhou — freely accessible.
  • Food Tip: Zhengzhou's huimian (braised noodle soup) is the local comfort food. Try it at any local restaurant.
