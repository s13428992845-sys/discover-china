Jilin City

A picturesque city on the Songhua River — famous for winter rime ice and mountain scenery.

Home / Jilin Province / Jilin City

City Overview

Jilin City is the only city in China that shares its name with its province. Set along the Songhua River and surrounded by mountains, it's famous for the spectacular winter rime ice (雾凇) phenomenon — when freezing fog coats the riverside trees in white crystals, creating a fairy-tale landscape.

📍 Location

Eastern Jilin Province, on the Songhua River. 30 min by HSR from Changchun.

❄️ Rime Ice

The famous 'Jilin rime' (雾凇) — freezing fog creates crystal-coated trees along the Songhua River.

🏔️ Songhua Lake

A massive lake surrounded by mountains — summer boating, winter ice fishing.

🌲 Beishan Park

A scenic hill park with temples and panoramic views of the city and river.

Top Attractions

❄️ Rime Ice Viewing

In January-February, the riverside trees are coated in white crystal rime — a magical natural phenomenon.

🏔️ Songhua Lake

Summer swimming and boating; winter ice fishing and snow activities.

🏛️ Beishan Park

Ancient temples on a hilltop — panoramic views of the city, river, and mountains.

🌿 Wula Mountain

A nearby scenic area with hiking trails and winter skiing opportunities.

Best Time to Visit Jilin City

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming up after harsh winter. May is pleasant. Ice melts on Songhua Lake.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Warm (22-28°C). Songhua Lake is perfect for swimming and boating.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Oct)

Beautiful fall colors. Mountain scenery is spectacular. Comfortable weather.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Very cold (-20 to -10°C). RIME ICE SEASON — the city's most famous attraction.

Jilin City Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Rime Ice: Best viewed in January-February along the Songhua River, especially early morning (6-10am).
  • Songhua Lake: In summer, rent a boat. In winter, try ice fishing — a unique local experience.
  • Beishan Park: Climb to the temple at the top for the best view of the city and river.
  • Food: Try 吉林煎粉 (Jilin fried noodles) — a local street food specialty.
