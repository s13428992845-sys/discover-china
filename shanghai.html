Shanghai

China's most cosmopolitan city — where Art Deco meets futuristic skylines, world-class dining lines historic lanes, and East truly meets West.

Home / Provinces / Shanghai

Province Overview

Shanghai is China's largest city and its most international. The Bund's colonial-era grandeur faces Pudong's soaring modern skyline across the Huangpu River. From trendy French Concession lanes to ancient water towns nearby, Shanghai offers an unparalleled urban experience blending old and new.

📍 Location

East China, at the mouth of the Yangtze River. China's largest city and global financial hub.

👥 Population

Approximately 26 million. China's most populous city and one of the world's largest.

🌡️ Climate

Subtropical. Hot humid summers, chilly winters. Best: Mar-May and Sep-Nov. Plum rain season in June.

🏔️ Terrain

Flat alluvial plain at Yangtze River delta. Average elevation 4m. Huangpu River divides the city.

🍜 Cuisine

Xiaolongbao (soup dumplings), shengjianbao, hairy crab, Shanghainese noodles, and world-class international dining.

✈️ Major Airport

Pudong (PVG) for international, Hongqiao (SHA) for domestic. Both well-connected to the city.

Why Visit Shanghai?

🌃 The Bund

Iconic waterfront with 52 historic buildings spanning Neo-Classical, Art Deco, and Baroque styles. Spectacular at night.

🏙️ Pudong Skyline

Shanghai Tower (632m), Oriental Pearl Tower, and Jin Mao Tower create one of the world's most dramatic skylines.

🌳 French Concession

Tree-lined avenues, boutique shops, cafes, and galleries in historic European-style neighborhood.

🏮 Yu Garden

400-year-old classical Chinese garden in the Old City. Exquisite rockeries, ponds, and pavilions.

🎨 M50 Art District

Former textile mills housing contemporary art galleries. Shanghai's creative heartbeat.

🏪 Nanjing Road

China's premier shopping street stretching 5.5km. From luxury brands to local specialties.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (March - May)

Cherry blossoms and pleasant weather. Great for walking the French Concession and parks.

☀️ Summer (June - August)

Very hot and humid, 35°C+. Plum rains in June. Best for indoor attractions and nightlife.

🍂 Autumn (September - November)

Perfect weather, clear skies. Best season for photography and outdoor exploration.

❄️ Winter (December - February)

Cold and damp, 0-8°C. Fewer tourists. Great for shopping, dining, and indoor culture.

Getting Around Shanghai

✈️ By Air

  • Pudong (PVG): Main international gateway, 40km east of center. Maglev train to city in 8 minutes
  • Hongqiao (SHA): Closer to city center, mostly domestic. Connected to subway and HSR station
  • Maglev: World's fastest commercial train — 431km/h from Pudong Airport to Longyang Road

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: To Beijing (4.5h), Hangzhou (1h), Nanjing (1.5h), and all major cities
  • Hongqiao Station: One of Asia's largest transport hubs, connected to airport and metro
  • Shanghai Station: Central location, serves some northern routes

🚇 By Metro

  • Network: 20 lines, 800+ km — world's longest metro system
  • Coverage: Reaches virtually every corner of the city and suburbs
  • Maglev: 431km/h from Pudong Airport. A must-experience engineering marvel

🚕 By Taxi

  • Availability: Millions of taxis. Use Didi app for easy hailing
  • Pricing: Starting at 14 CNY. Reasonable for city travel
  • Traffic: Allow extra time during rush hours (7-9AM, 5-7PM)

Shanghai Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Maglev Experience: Take the Maglev from Pudong Airport — 431km/h, 8 minutes. Sit on the right side for city views.
  • Bund Timing: Visit the Bund at sunset to see the skyline transition from day to night lights.
  • Xiaolongbao Rules: Bite a small hole, sip the broth first, then eat. Don't just bite into them or you'll burn your mouth!
  • French Concession: Rent a bike and explore the tree-lined streets. Start at Wukang Road and Fuxing Road.
  • Shopping: Nanjing Road for mainstream brands, Xintiandi for upscale, Tianzifang for artsy crafts.
  • Disney: Shanghai Disney is unique — has attractions found nowhere else. Book skip-the-line passes.
  • Water Towns: Zhujiajiao or Wuzhen make excellent day trips. Go early to avoid crowds.
  • Payment: Cash rarely used. Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay before arrival.
