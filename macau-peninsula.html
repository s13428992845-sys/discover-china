Macau Peninsula

The historic heart of Macau — UNESCO World Heritage sites, Portuguese charm, and vibrant culture.

Home / Macau / Macau Peninsula

City Overview

The Macau Peninsula is the original and historic core of Macau — a compact area where 400 years of Portuguese colonial heritage meets Chinese tradition. The UNESCO-listed Historic Centre includes the Ruins of St. Paul's, Senado Square, Guia Fortress, and dozens of churches and temples. It's one of the most walkable and atmospheric cities in Asia.

📍 Location

The northern part of Macau, connected to Zhuhai (mainland China) by a border crossing.

🏛️ UNESCO Heritage

The Historic Centre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site — one of the best-preserved colonial cities in Asia.

🏯 Ruins of St. Paul's

The iconic facade of a 17th-century church — Macau's most photographed landmark.

🏰 Senado Square

The heart of colonial Macau — wave-pattern mosaic paving surrounded by pastel-colored buildings.

Top Attractions

🏯 Ruins of St. Paul's

The grand facade rises dramatically above the street — climb the stairs for a close-up view. Free.

🏰 Senado Square

Walk the wave-pattern mosaic paving — surrounded by colonial buildings now housing shops and cafes.

🏛️ Guia Fortress

Climb to Macau's highest point — a 17th-century fortress with a chapel and the oldest lighthouse on the China coast.

🏯 A-Ma Temple

Macau's oldest temple (1488) — the name 'Macau' may have come from this temple (A-Ma-Gao).

Best Time to Visit Macau Peninsula

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm and humid. Pleasant for walking the historic streets. Some rain.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid. Indoor cafes and casinos offer cool refuge. Typhoon risk.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable. Perfect for walking the UNESCO heritage trail.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cool and dry. Comfortable for sightseeing. Fewer tourists. Great photography light.

Macau Peninsula Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • UNESCO Trail: Follow the marked Heritage Route — it covers all major sites in 2-3 hours of walking.
  • Ruins at Dawn: Visit St. Paul's before 9am to avoid crowds — the morning light is beautiful for photos.
  • Egg Tarts: Lord Stow's Bakery on Rua do Tapanoca invented the Macau egg tart — try the original.
  • Free Shuttles: Use the casino shuttle buses — they're free, air-conditioned, and connect all major areas.
