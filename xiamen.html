Xiamen

A subtropical island city of colonial architecture, beaches, and Gulangyu's car-free charm.

Home / Fujian Province / Xiamen

City Overview

Xiamen is one of China's most livable cities — a subtropical island connected to the mainland by bridges. Its highlight is Gulangyu, a UNESCO-listed car-free island of colonial architecture and piano music. The city's Buddhist Nanputuo Temple, coastal cycling paths, and seafood markets add to its charm.

📍 Location

Southeastern Fujian coast, facing Taiwan. A special economic zone since 1980.

🏝️ Gulangyu Island

UNESCO World Heritage — a car-free island of colonial villas and piano music.

🙏 Nanputuo Temple

A 1,000-year-old Buddhist temple at the foot of Wulao Peak — sea views from the top.

🚴 Island Cycling

A 43km环岛路 (island ring road) — one of China's best coastal cycling routes.

Top Attractions

🏝️ Gulangyu Island

Ferry to the car-free island — colonial architecture, piano museum, seaside beauty.

🙏 Nanputuo Temple

Climb Wulao Peak for panoramic views — the temple's Buddhist cuisine is excellent.

🚴 Island Ring Road

Cycle the 43km coastal road — ocean views, beaches, and tropical plants.

🏛️ Jimei School Village

A unique complex of Chinese-Western fusion architecture built by philanthropist Tan Kah Kee.

Best Time to Visit Xiamen

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warm and pleasant. Flowers blooming. Perfect for cycling and island exploration.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid. Beach season. Typhoon risk. Gulangyu is a refreshing escape.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable temperatures. Ideal for all activities.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Mild (10-18°C). Perfectly visitable. Few tourists. Pleasant walks.

Xiamen Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Gulangyu: Book ferry tickets at least 3 days ahead in peak season. The island has no cars.
  • Cycling: Rent a bike at the island ring road — the coastal stretch from Yefengzhai to Zengcuoan is best.
  • Food: Try 沙茶面 (satay noodles) and 海蛎煎 (oyster omelet) — Xiamen's signature dishes.
  • Best View: Climb Wulao Peak at Nanputuo Temple for a panorama of temple, city, and sea.
