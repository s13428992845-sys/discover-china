Tai'an (Mount Tai)

Gateway to sacred Mount Tai — climb 6,600 steps for sunrise views that emperors have sought for millennia.

Home / Shandong Province / Tai'an (Mount Tai)

City Overview

Tai'an is a small city at the base of Mount Tai (Tai Shan) — China's most sacred mountain. For 3,000 years, Chinese emperors climbed its 6,600 stone steps to worship heaven. Today, it remains one of China's most spiritually and physically rewarding experiences.

📍 Location

Central Shandong, at the southern base of Mount Tai. 1 hour by train from Jinan.

⛰️ Sacred Mountain

Mount Tai — 1,545m, the most sacred of China's Five Great Mountains. 6,600 stone steps to the summit.

🏛️ Emperor Worship

72 emperors performed the Feng Shan ceremony here — praying to heaven on the summit for 3,000 years.

🌅 Sunrise

Watching sunrise from the summit after an overnight climb is one of China's most iconic experiences.

Top Attractions

⛰️ Summit Climb

6,600 steps from the south gate to the Jade Emperor Peak (1,545m). Takes 4-6 hours up.

🌅 Sunrise View

The sunrise from the summit is legendary. Arrive by 5AM to secure a spot at the observation point.

🏛️ Dai Temple

At the mountain's base — the largest and best-preserved ancient temple complex on Mount Tai. 2,000 years old.

🚡 Cable Car

For those who prefer not to climb all 6,600 steps — cable car from Midway Gate to near the summit.

Best Time to Visit Tai'an (Mount Tai)

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming up. Green vegetation returning. Fewer crowds than summer. Good for clear summit views.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Peak season. Warm at base but cool at summit. Overnight climbs are popular. Book early.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Cool, clear skies, spectacular fall colors. Best chance of clear sunrise.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold and icy (-15 to 0°C at summit). Snow-covered mountain is dramatic but slippery.

Tai'an (Mount Tai) Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Overnight Climb: Start at 11PM, arrive summit by 5AM for sunrise. Bring headlamp, warm clothes, and water.
  • Warm Clothes: Summit is 15°C colder than base. Even in summer, bring a jacket for the sunrise wait.
  • Dai Temple: Visit before climbing — it sets the historical and spiritual context for the mountain.
  • Cable Car Down: Climb up, cable car down — saves your knees and still gives you the summit experience.
