Wuzhen

China's most beautiful water town — ancient canals, stone bridges, and the venue of the World Internet Conference.

Home / Zhejiang Province / Wuzhen

City Overview

Wuzhen is the crown jewel of China's water towns — a 1,300-year-old town of canals, stone bridges, and whitewashed houses. It's been beautifully preserved and restored, offering an authentic glimpse of ancient Jiangnan life.

📍 Location

Northern Zhejiang, between Hangzhou and Suzhou. About 1.5 hours from either city by car.

🏘️ Water Town

Classic Jiangnan water town — canals, stone bridges, wooden houses, and gondola rides.

💻 Tech Meets Tradition

Host of the World Internet Conference — where ancient architecture meets cutting-edge technology.

🏛️ Cultural Hub

Museums, traditional workshops (indigo dyeing, rice wine brewing), and folk performances.

Top Attractions

🏘️ East Gate (Dongzha)

The more commercialized but lively section — shops, restaurants, and the famous morning market scene.

🏘️ West Gate (Xizha)

The more authentic and atmospheric section — best preserved, beautiful at night with lantern lighting.

🛶 Gondola Ride

Traditional wooden boat rides through the narrow canals — the quintessential water town experience.

🎨 Indigo Workshop

Watch artisans create traditional blue-and-white fabric using centuries-old techniques.

Best Time to Visit Wuzhen

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Pleasant and atmospheric. Wisteria hangs over the canals. Fewer tourists than autumn.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot but the water town has a cooling effect. Evening visits are magical.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Cool weather, clear reflections in the canals. Beautiful lantern-lit nights.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Cold but atmospheric. Misty mornings over the canals. Very few tourists.

Wuzhen Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Stay Overnight: The real magic of Wuzhen is after the day-trippers leave. Stay inside the scenic area.
  • West Gate Priority: Visit Xizha (West Gate) first — it's more authentic and less commercialized.
  • Early Morning: Wake before 7AM to see the water town without crowds — mist on the canals is magical.
  • Rice Wine: Try the local rice wine — Wuzhen has been brewing it for centuries. Visit a traditional brewery.
