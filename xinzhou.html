Xinzhou (Wutai Shan)

Gateway to Mount Wutai — one of Buddhism's four sacred mountains with ancient temples and monasteries.

Home / Shanxi Province / Xinzhou (Wutai Shan)

City Overview

Mount Wutai (Wutai Shan) is one of China's Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains, dedicated to Manjushri (wisdom). With over 50 temples spanning from the Tang Dynasty to Qing, some housing China's oldest surviving wooden buildings, it's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

📍 Location

Northeastern Shanxi, in the Wutai Mountain range. 3,056m peak. 4-5 hours from Taiyuan.

🏛️ Sacred Mountain

One of Buddhism's four sacred mountains — dedicated to Manjushri (Wenshu), the Bodhisattva of Wisdom.

🏛️ Ancient Wood

Home to China's oldest surviving wooden buildings — Tang Dynasty Foguang Temple (857 AD).

🏔️ Five Terraces

The five flat-topped peaks give the mountain its name. North Terrace is the highest at 3,061m.

Top Attractions

🏛️ Foguang Temple

Built 857 AD — China's oldest surviving wooden building. Tang Dynasty architecture at its finest.

🏛️ Xiantong Temple

The largest and oldest temple on Mount Wutai — founded in the Eastern Han Dynasty (68 AD).

🏔️ Five Terraces Trek

Hike across the five flat-topped peaks — 2-3 days of alpine trekking with stunning views and remote temples.

🙏 Pilgrim Life

Join Tibetan Buddhist monks and pilgrims for morning chanting, circumambulation, and meditation.

Best Time to Visit Xinzhou (Wutai Shan)

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Snow melting. Temple renovations begin. Few pilgrims — peaceful atmosphere.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Best season. Green mountains, comfortable 15-20°C. Peak pilgrimage season. July is busiest.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Spectacular fall colors. Cool temperatures. Fewer crowds. Perfect for temple photography.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Very cold (-20 to -5°C). Snow-covered temples are stunning. Few visitors but magical.

Xinzhou (Wutai Shan) Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Allow 2 Days: There are too many temples for a day trip. Stay overnight in the town at the mountain's base.
  • Foguang Temple: This Tang Dynasty building is 1,167 years old. It's 50km from the main town — arrange transport.
  • Five Terrace Trek: The full circuit takes 2-3 days. Bring camping gear or stay in mountain temples.
  • Respect Monks: Many temples are active monasteries. Walk clockwise, remove hats, ask before photographing monks.
