Qinghai Province

The Tibetan Plateau's jewel — vast Qinghai Lake, sacred monasteries, and surreal salt lakes in otherworldly landscapes.

Home / Provinces / Qinghai Province

Province Overview

The Tibetan Plateau's jewel — vast Qinghai Lake, sacred monasteries, and surreal salt lakes in otherworldly landscapes.

📍 Location

Northeastern Tibetan Plateau. China's largest province by area. Average elevation 3,000m+.

👥 Population

About 6 million — one of China's least populated provinces. Tibetan, Hui, Han, Mongu, and Salar peoples.

🌡️ Climate

High-altitude continental. Cold and dry year-round. Summer (Jun-Aug) is the only warm season. UV radiation is intense.

🏔️ Terrain

Vast grasslands, snow-capped mountains, salt lakes, and the source of China's three major rivers: Yangtze, Yellow River, and Mekong.

🍜 Cuisine

Tibetan yak butter tea, tsampa (roasted barley), Hui Muslim mutton dishes, and Qinghai yogurt.

✈️ Major Airport

Xining Caojiabao (XNN) — connects to Beijing, Shanghai, Chengdu, and Xi'an.

Why Visit Qinghai Province?

🌊 Qinghai Lake

China's largest lake — a vast turquoise expanse of blue at 3,200m surrounded by grasslands, yaks, and prayer flags.

🧂 Chaka Salt Lake

The 'Mirror of the Sky' — a surreal white salt flat that perfectly reflects the sky. Photographer's paradise.

🏛️ Kumbum Monastery

One of Tibetan Buddhism's six major Gelug monasteries — birthplace of Tsongkhapa, founded in 1583.

🌻 Menyuan Rapeseed

In July, millions of acres of golden rapeseed flowers bloom against the Qilian Mountains — one of China's most spectacular landscapes.

🏔️ Yushu

Remote Tibetan prefecture with authentic nomadic culture, dramatic mountain scenery, and the source of the Mekong River.

🦅 Bird Island

A tiny island in Qinghai Lake hosting hundreds of thousands of migratory birds — a nature reserve and spectacle.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Still cold but warming. Grasslands begin to green. Few tourists and clear skies.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Best season. Warm days (15-25°C), rapeseed flowers in July, Qinghai Lake cycling events. Peak tourist season.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Golden grasslands and clear skies. Temperature drops rapidly after September. Fewer crowds.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Extremely cold (-15 to -25°C). Most attractions accessible but very challenging conditions.

Getting Around Qinghai Province

✈️ By Air

  • Airport: Xining airport connects to major Chinese cities. Limited flights to remote areas within Qinghai.

🚄 By Train

  • Railway: Xining connects by high-speed rail to Lanzhou (1h), Xi'an (4h), Lhasa (via the Qinghai-Tibet Railway — 22h).

🚌 By Bus

  • Bus: Buses connect major towns. Some remote areas require chartered vehicles.

🚗 By Car

  • Driving: Self-driving is the best way to explore. The Qinghai-Tibet Highway (G109) is one of the world's great road trips.

Qinghai Province Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Altitude Sickness: Xining is at 2,200m, Qinghai Lake at 3,200m. Spend 1-2 days acclimatizing. Take it easy.
  • Sun Protection: UV radiation is extreme at this altitude. Bring high-SPF sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses.
  • Best Time: July is ideal — warm weather, rapeseed flowers in bloom, and the Qinghai Lake cycling race.
  • Chaka Photos: Visit the salt lake at sunrise or sunset for perfect mirror reflections. Avoid windy days.
  • Respect Tibetan Culture: Walk clockwise around stupas and monasteries. Ask before photographing monks.
  • Warm Clothing: Even in summer, nights can drop to 5°C. Bring layers regardless of season.
  • Fuel Up: Gas stations are sparse in western Qinghai. Fill up whenever you can on long drives.
  • Qinghai-Tibet Train: The train from Xining to Lhasa is one of the world's great rail journeys. Book a soft sleeper.
