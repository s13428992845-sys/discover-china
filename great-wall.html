Great Wall (Beijing Sections)

Walk along one of humanity's greatest engineering achievements — multiple accessible sections near Beijing offer hiking for all levels.

Home / Beijing / Great Wall (Beijing Sections)

City Overview

The Great Wall near Beijing offers several sections to explore, each with different characteristics. From restored and tourist-friendly Badaling to wild and adventurous Jianshanling, there's a section for every traveler. The wall winds through dramatic mountain landscapes, offering both historical insight and spectacular views.

📍 Location

Northern Beijing mountains. 60-130km from city center depending on section.

📏 Length

The Beijing sections span hundreds of kilometers. Individual sections range from 2-20km.

🏔️ Elevation

600-1,000m above sea level. Moderate to strenuous hiking with many steps.

🏛️ History

Most visible sections are Ming Dynasty (1368-1644), built to defend against northern invaders.

Top Attractions

🧱 Mutianyu

The best section for most visitors — beautifully restored, scenic, with cable car and toboggan options.

🏔️ Jinshanling

For serious hikers — 10km of wild and restored wall with incredible mountain views. Less crowded.

📸 Simatai

The most photogenic section — wild wall against dramatic mountain backdrops. Night tours available.

🎯 Badaling

The most accessible and famous section. Very crowded but well-restored. Good for families.

Best Time to Visit Great Wall (Beijing Sections)

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Wildflowers along the wall. Pleasant hiking temperatures. Some sections can be slippery.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and crowded. Start early to avoid heat and masses. Bring plenty of water.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

BEST season — spectacular fall foliage along the wall. Clear mountain air.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Snow-covered wall is stunning but slippery and very cold. For experienced hikers only.

Great Wall (Beijing Sections) Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Which Section: First-timers: Mutianyu. Hikers: Jinshanling. Photographers: Simatai. Families: Badaling.
  • Go Early: Leave Beijing by 6-7AM to arrive before tour buses. Mutianyu at sunrise is unforgettable.
  • Proper Footwear: Hiking shoes essential. The steps are uneven and can be very steep (up to 70°).
  • Bring Supplies: Water, snacks, and sun protection. Prices on the wall are expensive.
