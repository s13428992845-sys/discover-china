Hohhot

The "Green City" — Inner Mongolia's capital, where golden Tibetan Buddhist temples meet endless blue skies and the vast grasslands begin.

Home / Inner Mongolia / Hohhot

📍 Top Attractions

Dazhao Temple (大召寺)

The oldest and largest Tibetan Buddhist temple in Hohhot, built in 1580. Features a stunning silver Buddha statue and intricate murals.

Inner Mongolia Museum

One of China's best regional museums — dinosaur fossils, Genghis Khan exhibits, and fascinating displays of nomadic culture and history.

Five Pagoda Temple (五塔寺)

A unique 18th-century Buddhist temple with a rare astronomical stone carving map featuring Mongolian script.

🍜 Food & Cuisine

  • Mongolian Hotpot (涮羊肉) — Paper-thin lamb slices in copper hotpot
  • Shaomai (烧麦) — Hohhot's signature breakfast: steamed dumplings with lamb and scallions
  • Milk tea (奶茶) — Salty Mongolian-style milk tea with millet
  • Dairy products — Yogurt, milk skin, and cheese unique to Inner Mongolia

🚄 Getting There

✈️ By Air

  • Hohhot Baita International Airport (HET)
  • Flights from Beijing (~1.5h), major Chinese cities

🚄 By High-Speed Rail

  • Beijing to Hohhot: ~2.5 hours
  • Convenient connections to Baotou and Ordos

💡 Travel Tips

  • Best time: July–August for pleasant weather and Naadam Festival season
  • Hohhot is the gateway to Xilamuren and Huitengxile grasslands (1-2 hours away)
  • Try local dairy products — milk skin and yogurt are among China's best
  • Dress in layers — temperatures can swing significantly between day and night
