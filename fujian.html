Fujian Province

Maritime heritage, mysterious Tulou roundhouses, Wuyishan tea culture, and the cosmopolitan charm of Xiamen.

Home / Provinces / Fujian Province

Province Overview

Maritime heritage, mysterious Tulou roundhouses, Wuyishan tea culture, and the cosmopolitan charm of Xiamen.

📍 Location

Southeastern China, facing Taiwan across the strait. Borders Zhejiang, Jiangxi, and Guangdong.

👥 Population

Approximately 42 million. Famous overseas Chinese homeland with deep maritime trading heritage.

🌡️ Climate

Subtropical monsoon. Warm and humid year-round. Typhoon season Jul-Sep. Best visited Oct-Apr.

🏔️ Terrain

80% mountains and hills. Famous for Wuyishan in the north, Tulou in the west, and the rocky Fujian coast.

🍜 Cuisine

Min cuisine — one of China's Eight Great Cuisines. Seafood, soups, Buddha Jumps Over the Wall, and oyster omelettes.

✈️ Major Airport

Xiamen Gaoqi (XMN) and Fuzhou Changle (FOC) — both with extensive domestic and international routes.

Why Visit Fujian Province?

🏠 Fujian Tulou

UNESCO World Heritage — massive circular earthen communal houses housing entire clans. Unique to Fujian's Hakka people.

🍵 Wuyishan

Birthplace of oolong tea and Zhengshan Xiaozhong (first black tea). Dramatic Danxia landscape and ancient tea villages.

🏝️ Gulangyu Island

UNESCO World Heritage — Xiamen's car-free island with colonial architecture, piano museum, and subtropical gardens.

🏛️ Quanzhou

Marco Polo's 'Zaiton' — once the world's largest port. Ancient mosques, temples, and Maritime Silk Road heritage.

🌊 Taimu Mountain

Granite sea-eroded landscape with caves, peaks, and coastal views — Fujian's hidden geological gem.

🏖️ Pingtan Island

Fujian's largest island — pristine beaches, unique stone houses, and the mysterious 'blue tears' bioluminescence.

Best Time to Visit

🌸 Spring (Mar-May)

Warming and pleasant. Tea picking season at Wuyishan (April-May). Gulangyu's flowers bloom beautifully.

☀️ Summer (Jun-Aug)

Hot and humid (33°C+). Typhoon risk. Beach weather but crowded. Wuyishan is cooler.

🍂 Autumn (Sep-Nov)

Best season. Clear skies, comfortable temps, tea harvest. Perfect for Tulou and Quanzhou visits.

❄️ Winter (Dec-Feb)

Mild (8-15°C). Perfect for sightseeing without crowds. Wuyishan is quiet and atmospheric.

Getting Around Fujian Province

✈️ By Air

  • Airports: Xiamen Gaoqi (XMN) is the main gateway. Fuzhou Changle (FOC), Quanzhou Jinjiang (JJN), and Wuyishan (WUS) airports also available.

🚄 By Train

  • High-Speed Rail: Xiamen to Fuzhou (1.5h), Shenzhen (3h), Shanghai (5h). Coastal HSR line is scenic.
  • Wuyishan: Accessible by HSR from Fuzhou (1.5h) or Shanghai (3.5h).

🚌 By Bus

  • Tulou Access: Buses from Xiamen to Nanjing county (Tulou area, 3h). Tourist buses also available.
  • Local: City buses connect major attractions.

🚕 By Taxi

  • Availability: Taxis and Didi widely available. Xiamen is very walkable.
  • Pricing: Starting at 10 CNY. Gulangyu is car-free — explore on foot.

Fujian Province Travel Tips

⚠️ Important Reminders

  • Gulangyu: Buy ferry tickets online days ahead — limited daily visitors. Allow a full day. Don't miss the Piano Museum.
  • Tulou Visit: The Chengqi Lou (largest roundhouse) is in Yongding. Yunshuiyao village is the most scenic. Allow 2 days from Xiamen.
  • Wuyishan Tea: Visit a real tea farm in the Nine-Bend Creek area. Taste freshly roasted Da Hong Pao oolong.
  • Quanzhou: Rent a bike to explore this flat city. The mix of Buddhist, Islamic, Hindu, and Christian sites is unique.
  • Blue Tears: Pingtan Island's bioluminescent 'blue tears' appear May-August on dark nights. A natural wonder.
  • Food Guide: Try Buddha Jumps Over the Wall (expensive but iconic), oyster omelettes, and Fujian fish balls.
  • Xiamen University: One of China's most beautiful campuses. Visit the芙蓉隧道 (Furong Tunnel) student art gallery.
  • Payment: Cash rarely used. Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay before arrival.
