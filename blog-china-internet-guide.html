🌐 Understanding China's Internet Environment

China operates its own internet infrastructure, separate from the global internet. This means some services you use daily at home won't work in China unless you prepare.

❌ What's Blocked in China

🚫 Social Media

  • Facebook, Instagram, Twitter
  • WhatsApp, Messenger
  • Telegram, Signal
  • Reddit, Pinterest, Tumblr

🚫 Google Services

  • Google Search (partial)
  • Gmail
  • Google Maps, Google Drive
  • Google Play Store
  • YouTube

🚫 Other Services

  • Netflix, Spotify (partially)
  • Wikipedia (Chinese blocked)
  • Many news sites
  • Dropbox (unreliable)

✅ What Works

  • Chinese equivalents: WeChat, Weibo
  • Apple App Store (iOS)
  • Banking & email (VPN needed)
  • Hotel WiFi (VPN needed for most)

Does this mean you'll be cut off? Not if you plan ahead. A VPN restores access to blocked services, and Chinese apps work well for local services.

📱 Option 1: eSIM (Recommended for Most)

eSIM is the easiest, most convenient option for travelers to China in 2026. It's embedded in your phone, no physical SIM card to swap, and works immediately upon landing.

Why Choose eSIM?

✅ Advantages of eSIM

  • Instant activation — activate before you even board the plane
  • No physical SIM — don't lose or swap SIMs at the airport
  • Flexible plans — choose duration and data amount
  • Easy to add — online purchase, QR code activation
  • Keep your home number — dual SIM functionality (eSIM + physical)

Setting Up eSIM

  1. Check if your phone supports eSIM (most iPhones XS+ and many Androids do)
  2. Buy an eSIM plan online (Airalo, Nomad, etc.) before departure
  3. Download the eSIM app and activate your plan
  4. Scan the QR code to add the eSIM to your phone
  5. Enable data roaming when you land in China

⚠️ Important: Not All eSIMs Work in China

Avoid "global" eSIMs that promise one SIM for all countries. These often don't work in China due to local regulations. Buy a China-specific eSIM from the providers above.

📶 Option 2: Physical SIM Card

If your phone doesn't support eSIM or you prefer a physical SIM, buying one at the airport or from a Chinese carrier is straightforward.

Buying a SIM at the Airport

Major international airports (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Xi'an) have SIM card kiosks. Here's what to expect:

🛬 Airport SIM Buying Guide

  1. Locate the kiosk — Look for "China Mobile" or "China Unicom" signs in international arrivals
  2. Passport required — You must show your passport for registration
  3. Choose a plan — Tourist packages typically ¥100-200 for 5-10GB, 30 days
  4. Activate immediately — Staff will insert the SIM and verify it works
  5. Keep the SIM card — Some airports require you to register it on a machine before leaving

💰 Cost Estimates (Physical SIM)

  • SIM card: Free (included with plan) or ¥10-30
  • 7-day tourist plan: ¥100-150 (~$15-22) for 5GB
  • 30-day tourist plan: ¥150-200 (~$22-30) for 10GB
  • Extra data: ¥50-100/GB (can buy more later)

📡 Option 3: Pocket WiFi

Pocket WiFi devices are rental hotspots you can pick up at the airport or have delivered to your hotel. They're useful if:

  • You have multiple devices (phone + tablet + laptop)
  • Your phone doesn't support eSIM
  • You want to share internet with travel companions
  • You want to avoid buying a SIM card

Pocket WiFi providers:

  • Airport rental services — ¥50-100/day, battery life 8-12 hours
  • Online rental — Skyroam, Tep Wireless (book before you go)
  • Hotel rental — Some hotels offer pocket WiFi rentals

Drawbacks:

  • Need to carry another device and charger
  • More expensive than SIM cards
  • Battery life is limited
  • Connection quality varies by location

🏨 Using Hotel and Free WiFi

Most hotels, hostels, and cafés in major Chinese cities offer free WiFi. However:

  • Hotel WiFi is typically password-protected (check your key card)
  • VPN required for most services (blocked sites won't work even on hotel WiFi)
  • Speed varies — boutique hotels may have slower connections
  • Not reliable for data-heavy activities like streaming

Pro tip: Take screenshots of WiFi passwords at your hotel, café, and public spaces. Ask staff in Chinese ("WIFI mima?" — "WiFi password?") if you can't connect.

🔒 VPN Guide: Staying Secure & Connected

What is a VPN?

A Virtual Private Network routes your internet through a server outside China, bypassing the firewall. This restores access to blocked sites and encrypts your connection.

VPN Legality in China

⚖️ Legal Status

VPNs are technically illegal for unregistered commercial use. However:

  • Individual tourists are rarely targeted
  • Most hotels cater to international guests and assume VPN usage
  • Enforcement varies by region and political climate
  • Avoid discussing VPNs publicly or posting about them
  • Use established VPN providers, not obscure free services

VPN Tips for China

  • Activate before arrival — set up and test your VPN on your home network
  • Download offline mode — ExpressVPN and NordVPN have offline activation options
  • Have multiple servers — Hong Kong, Japan, and US servers work well
  • Keep it updated — VPN apps update protocols to stay ahead of blocks
  • Don't rely on free VPNs — they often don't work in China and may compromise security
  • Use "auto-connect" mode — VPNs that auto-reconnect when the connection drops

🗺️ Internet in Different Parts of China

Internet quality varies significantly by region:

  • Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen: Excellent 5G coverage, fast speeds, reliable
  • Chengdu, Xi'an, Hangzhou: Very good coverage, some rural slowdowns
  • Yunnan Province — including Lijiang, Dali: Good city coverage, mountain areas have weak signals
  • Remote areas (Tibet, Xinjiang, far west): Limited to no 4G, satellite connections available

📋 Pre-Departure Checklist

  • ⬜ Download and test a VPN
  • ⬜ Buy a China eSIM (Airalo, Nomad) or plan for airport SIM
  • ⬜ Download offline maps (Maps.me, China region)
  • ⬜ Download translation apps (Pleco, Baidu Translate)
  • ⬜ Download Didi (ride-hailing app)
  • ⬜ Download travel apps (Agoda, Booking.com)
  • ⬜ Save hotel addresses in Chinese characters (for maps and taxis)
  • ⬜ Test your phone's international roaming settings
  • ⬜ Bring a portable charger (power bank)
  • ⬜ Note: Google Maps works only with VPN in China
